The founding model — non-linear, softly gated

Founding a town requires only that it is reachable on the map and that you can afford the rising caravan cost (a basket of goods that scales per founding). There is no "finish this town before founding the next." On top of that open base sit a few light, acyclic, pacing-oriented soft gates — never the old prerequisite chain.

① Board-feed dependency

You can't run a mine until you own a farm (mines eat food as expedition turns), nor a fish board until you own a farm + mine (bait + tackle). This is the same stacking shown in the Overview — the dependency that makes later boards feel earned.

② Low home-tier nudge

The first combination town asks home ≥ Hamlet; the first hazard town asks home ≥ Village. A gentle nudge so the farm loop is learned before the mine arrives — not a wall.

③ Rising caravan cost

Founding is a resource caravan (farm goods early, +metal later) scaling ~×1.6 per town, so the frontier unfurls outward over time. Coins fund session tolls and wages, not founding — a deliberate demand on your production, per the Zones 1–2 baseline.

④ The finale gate

The only hard gate: the Golden Capital's finale opens once every required-spine town is complete. This replaces the legacy "3 Hearth-Tokens" gate (see decision log).

The only dependencies are acyclic and feed-based. Caravan cost + map reach handle the rest of the pacing.

The no-softlock invariant

A gating resource must be producible by the rung before it is spent. Every tier-up cost and founding cost may only demand resources the player can already make at that point — either a base/board resource of a board they own, or a crafted good whose station unlocks on an earlier rung. This is adopted verbatim from the Zone Atlas build rule, and the progression oracle checks it every run.

This invariant is what makes "non-linear + soft gates" safe. The historical softlock — home's Hamlet rung once demanded bread, whose Bakery demanded the mine good block, gated behind a mine gated behind the home climb — was exactly an invariant violation. It was re-costed onto farm goods on 2026-06-23 (now decision-log history); the canonical rule prevents the whole class.

The home ladder — Hearthwood Vale

What a tier-up does (every settlement)

  • Raises the plot count — more room for buildings, the core scarcity lever.
  • Unlocks building types — new stations, houses, and the town's signature export building.
  • Levels the landmark in place — the town's central structure visibly transforms (see each town's landmark stages in the Atlas), so growth reads at a glance.
  • Does not change the puzzle math. Turn budget and yield are untouched by tiering; what changes is what you can build with the room you've won.
Buildings gate what the board spawns. Raising a station also turns on its tile category — e.g. a Coop makes bird tiles start appearing. So your build choices steer the board's output. Full mechanics in Systems.

The finale

When every required-spine town is complete, the Golden Capital opens its capstone. The finale is narrative + payoff, not a new board type: a short run of story beats, a choice-reactive epilogue, and the build of the Monument that wakes the kingdom — plus a capstone reward (coins + a title/cosmetic) and a place in the Chronicle. The Capital itself plays all three boards at the grandest scale on the way there.

Supersedes two legacy conditions. The old 3 Hearth-Tokens unlock (one per biome) and the act3_win festival-larder terminal beat are both retired in favour of "complete the required spine → build the Monument." Recorded in the decision log; the token plumbing remains usable as optional flavour.
