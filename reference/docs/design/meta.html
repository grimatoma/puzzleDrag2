Story & the Chronicle

The narrative is delivered in beats through a story modal at milestone moments (founding a town, beating a boss, raising a landmark). Beats are choice-reactive — your picks colour later prose and land in the Chronicle, a timeline of your playthrough. The arc runs from the cold hearth at Hearthwood to the Monument at the Golden Capital; the finale is the capstone beat.

Quests & dailies

Rotating daily quests (chain N of a family, fill an order, raise a building) give bite-sized goals and small rewards. They steer attention without forcing a particular board, and feed the Goals hub below.

The login streak & daily rewards

A daily streak ticks once per calendar day (idempotent within a day) and pays escalating daily rewards — coins, tools, the occasional Villager. One of the between-session faucets that let a returning player go faster for free. half-wired: the streak reducer ships, but a dispatch site on app-mount is still needed (see Build status).

Civic economy — the Town Hall

A return-to-collect civic loop: founded settlements accrue a tithe in real time, claimable at the Town Hall on a cooldown, plus a provisions care-package of tools keyed to your buildings.

Milestones & the Goals hub

Milestone celebrations mark firsts — first building, first chain-of-10, first City tier, first town founded — with a toast + announcement + optional confetti, once each, recorded in a persisted seen-set (old saves seeded so they don't backlog-spam).

The Goals hub is a single panel aggregating the next pull-you-back targets: daily streak, next Almanac (XP) tier, next tier-up, active daily quests — reusing existing selectors. It surfaces the retention systems that are otherwise buried. to build — see project 12.

Bosses (the challenge wrap)

Bosses are detailed as a system and per-town in the Atlas; here they matter as a retention beat — the headline encounter each zone builds toward, with a story beat and a capstone reward on the win.

The free-to-play money model

Hybrid-casual: the game is fully playable free; spending buys time and shelf-space, never power.

Rewarded ads (opt-in)

  • Daily tool-crate
  • Skip a cooldown
  • Boost a win-task
  • Maybe: double a run's yield

No banners, ever.

IAP

  • Buy Villagers
  • Tool-crate bundles
  • Remove-ads
  • Cosmetics / titles (incl. the finale capstone)

Hybrid expansion

  • Optional expansion villages (extra optional-frontier content), bought as packs
  • Bounded cross-zone exports — no stat-stacking meta-tree
The line we don't cross. No mechanic may make a paying player stronger on the board than a free one who put in the effort — only faster or prettier. This keeps the "effort-gated, not time-gated" promise honest.
