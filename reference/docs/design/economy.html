Faucets ↔ sinks

Balance is the conversation between where value enters the economy (faucets) and where it leaves (sinks). The resource axis is where the old softlock lived; keeping faucets ahead of the sinks that gate them is the no-softlock invariant.

Faucets (value in)

  • Chain collection → materials (per TILES_PER_RESOURCE threshold)
  • Chain coins (linear in chain length)
  • Season-end bonus (+25 coins) & Chapel (+50 at season end)
  • Daily rewards, civic tithes, timed depots, boons

Sinks (value out)

  • Tier-up costs (materials; the main sink)
  • Founding cost (a resource caravan, paid in farm goods)
  • Session entry tolls (coins per visit)
  • Worker wages + hire ramps; crafting inputs; win-task baskets

The value-spread problem

Realized value-per-tile spans ~10× (pearls ≈ 133/tile vs pie ≈ 12.9/tile), with a worst-case outlier near 192×. Anything >3× the median is flagged. Target: compress to a 3–4× ceiling so no single tile family dominates income and every board stays worth playing.

The harness already emits a compression change-list (npm run playtest → change-list.json), applied through the Dev Panel cost-matrix. Deep tuning is deferred until the cross-zone pacing sim crosses boards (currently farm-only). Two levers move it: ITEMS[].value (sell worth) and TILES_PER_RESOURCE (the income divisor).

Workers & hiring

Each hire spends 1 Villager + coins + materials; coins ramp linearly per successive hire and materials ramp every 3 hires. A worker reduces a family's chain-to-collect threshold, capped at threshold − 3.

Rations → mine turns

A mine spends food as expedition turns. Raw produce works; crafted food gives more turns per unit (a soft efficiency gradient, never a hard requirement). Minimum to set out: 12 turns of packed food, plus the entry toll.

The price rule — one source of truth

Every sellable key has exactly one sell price: MARKET_PRICES[key].sell == ITEMS[key].value. A key must pay the same coins whether the UI classifies it as a "resource" or an "item." This retires the historical 10× fork (three disagreeing price sources: MARKET_PRICES, ITEMS.value, and a sellPriceFor 10%-of-value path). Implementation is tracked as project 13.

Open balance questions

  • Coin pacing. Coins reach a founding price in ~2 runs — too fast. Make coins a softer secondary gate (e.g. a per-action / entry sink) once progression is the primary gate.
  • Workers → chain length. Wire Houses → Villagers → shorter on-board chains (per-zone pool or global? magnitudes?). The top open lever.
  • Crafting substance. Should deeper refines gate on real-time timer, in-game-time, or a "juice" resource? Designer lean: in-game-time gate; basics stay instant.
  • Late-arc numbers. Towns 4–7 + frontier ladders are specced but unvalidated; the cross-zone pacing sim (currently farm-only) must cross boards to confirm.
  • Value-spread compression. Land the harness change-list once the price rule (project 13) merges, so both tables move in lockstep.
