The board & the chain

A turn is one chain: drag through 3+ adjacent matching tiles (8-direction, no reuse). At fixed thresholds along the chain an upgraded tile spawns and a resource is credited; the board collapses and refills. A visit spends a budget of turns (~10 at home) and banks its harvest into that town's stockpile. Raw goods refine at buildings into the materials that grow the town.

Cross-collect. A chain in one family can also credit a partner family on adjacent tiles (e.g. grass â†” grain, trees â†” birds), rewarding board-reading over rote single-family chains.

Tiles, families & promotion

Each tile belongs to a family with a chain-to-upgrade threshold. Long chains promote tiles up their family ladder, and every TILES_PER_RESOURCE tiles chained yields one unit of that family's resource.

Workers & hiring

Houses produce Villagers; hiring spends a Villager + coins + materials to place a worker that eases one family's chains. Caps and ramps per profession are in Economy â†’ Workers. Workers travel with you between towns; a town's materials do not.

Tools

Single-use board-breakers crafted at the Workshop (rake, sickle, plough, fruit-picker, fertilizer) or won from the between-session faucets (depots, daily, civic). Fertilizer doubles a visit's turn budget. The design principle: the wait between sessions hands you tools for free, or you craft them now by spending extra materials â€” never a hard time-gate.
