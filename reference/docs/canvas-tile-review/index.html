The verdict

The static art is the strong part. All 159 icons share one disciplined "glossy sticker" language — a dark outline, a consistent top-/top-left key light, gradient-shaded bodies, and a soft elliptical ground-shadow — so at rest the library reads as one cohesive family. The first-draft weakness is almost entirely motion. The set feels janky and cheap not because the drawings are bad but because nearly every icon fakes life the same few ways — a sliding glint, a glow pulse, a twinkle, some rising particles — laid over a body that never actually moves, and because none of the 12 animation principles (anticipation, squash & stretch, overshoot, eased timing, follow-through, secondary motion) are applied. A minority of icons are genuinely alive; they should become the benchmarks the rest are rebuilt against.

Cross-cutting findings

The patterns that repeat across modules — fix these once and dozens of icons improve.

1 · “Static body + animated overlay” — the #1 systemic failure

The object's geometry never deforms; life is faked with a glint/sheen sweep, a glow pulse, twinkles, or particles painted over a frozen shape. Pervasive across ores (all eight have a measured 0 px body delta), treasure, weapons (glint-only), the gem trio, drinks (rigid vessels), furniture (lamp/table/mirror/bookshelf never move — only their light does), nature (light-cycling), festive (holly/candy-cane), and several instruments. Fix: animate the OBJECT first (breathe / squash / bob / tilt) and demote the overlay to a secondary accent.

2 · Rigid whole-sprite rotation (the brief's named failure)

spell_wind, reef_starfish, astral_galaxy, arcane_pentacle, instr_bell, instr_tambourine, the weapon pendulums and bld_windmill (constant-velocity) all rotate the entire drawing about one pivot. It reads as a spinning logo/hubcap, not character — and on symmetric shapes (pentacle, starfish, galaxy) it's barely perceptible despite a high motion score. Fix: articulate (per-part motion, traveling waves, sensible pivots) instead of rotating the whole sprite.

3 · Dead / near-static icons (motion < ~3)

On the board these are indistinguishable from still images: bld_tower (0.1), bld_barn (0.17), drink_milk (0.57), furn_bookshelf (0.61), ore_crystal_vein (0.87), festive_holly (1.21), cozy_mailbox (1.22), treasure_map (1.63), weapon_dagger (1.69), weapon_mace (1.73), arcane_potion_blue (1.75), gem_emerald, arcane_runestone, wtool_nail. Several have animation code that is simply sub-perceptible at size.

4 · Blank rest frames — unusable as idle tiles

astral_shooting_star and weather_comet are completely EMPTY at frame 0 — the subject only exists mid-flight as it streaks across and off the canvas. A tile must always show its subject at rest. Fix: park the subject in-frame and make the streak a periodic event.

5 · None of the 12 animation principles (the root cause of “cheap”)

Almost every animation is a pure linear Math.sin(t·f) with no eased in/out, no anticipation wind-up, no overshoot/settle, no follow-through/overlap, and no squash & stretch. This single gap explains most of the “janky” feel. It is the highest-leverage thing to fix set-wide.

6 · Shadows don't react to motion

Ground-shadow ellipses stay frozen while subjects bob, hop, and breathe, so contact is broken (pets, crops, drinks, …). Fix: couple shadow scale/offset inversely to vertical motion — the one cheap change that grounds everything.

7 · Framing / centering is inconsistent

Everything is pinned to a y=22 shadow baseline regardless of subject height, so short subjects float with dead air above: treasure_gold_bars (31 u empty on top), wtool_drill / saw / wrench (off ~8–13 u), instr_fiddle (off_x 9), arcane_magic_dust (off_x 7.1), crop_peas (off_y 8.4), drink_tea (off_y 9.1). Fix: center each subject's bounding box; don't pin to a shared baseline.

8 · Scale / visual-weight drifts within and across modules

bug_dragonfly is a thin stick next to bug_snail's boulder; weapon_dagger is half the mass of the shield; the coffee/tea saucers read ~50% bulkier than the narrow drink glasses. Establish a target footprint band so a mixed board reads evenly.

9 · Motion-score caveat

A high motion number is NOT proof of good motion — a rigid rotation or a bright sliding glint rectangle inflates pixel-change while reading as lifeless. Judge perceived character, not the metric. (The metric is still great for finding the dead end of the scale.)

10 · Shared-helper bugs cause “jank” across many icons at once

treasure's glintSweep() emits a hard-edged rectangle that leaks past loose clips (a compositing-glitch look on coin_stack & chest); sweepPhase() in ores/treasure is a triangle wave that hard-resets (a teleport, not an eased return); buildings' flicker() only swings alpha 0.82–1.0 — imperceptible, yet it's the only animation on tower & barn. Fixing these helpers cleans up many icons in one move.

11 · Loop seams

Incommensurate sine frequencies or sawtooth resets break seamless looping (arcane_crystal_ball spin, the candle, several glint sweeps). Fix: drive loops off phase = 2π·f/loopLen so they tile exactly.

12 · The overlay tricks are over-used as a crutch

Glint-sweep + twinkle + glow-pulse appear in nearly every module, so even though the static art is nicely varied, the approach to motion feels samey across the whole set. Variety of gesture (peck, snip, ring, drip, flutter, jet) is what's missing.

The bar & direction

The target is playful, characterful idle motion with Warcraft-3 unit-idle energy — each idle a small gesture appropriate to the subject (a chicken pecks, a tree breezes, a gem glints-and-sparkles, a fish bobs & flicks, a bell rings). Idles need not be uniform, but they must share one consistent style and energy level. Three rules turn this set from first-draft to compelling:

  1. Animate the body, not the light. Lead with real deformation (breathe, squash & stretch, bob, tilt, articulate); keep the glint/twinkle/glow as a secondary accent, never the whole show.
  2. Apply the 12 principles. Every idle gets eased timing (not linear sine), anticipation before a beat, an overshoot + settle after it, and a touch of secondary/overlapping motion so parts trail the lead.
  3. Prefer event-driven beats over constant motion. A peck → rest → peck reads as alive; a perpetual metronome sine reads as a machine. Give most icons a rest pose and a periodic “moment.”

Shared standards to adopt set-wide: a small easing/helper library (easeInOut, easeOutBack for overshoot); a “shadow couples to vertical motion” rule; a centering + target-footprint pass; fixes to the shared glintSweep / sweepPhase / flicker helpers; a “never blank at rest” rule; and a “loop by phase” rule for seamless cycles.

Prioritized roadmap

P0 — Correctness (do first)

  • Fix the two blank rest frames (astral_shooting_star, weather_comet).
  • Resurrect the truly dead icons (tower, barn, milk, bookshelf, crystal_vein, holly, mailbox, map, dagger, mace, both potions, runestone, nail) — each needs a real, visible idle.
  • Fix the shared-helper bugs (glintSweep feather + tight clip + lower intensity; sweepPhase eased return; flicker amplitude) — high leverage, many icons at once.
  • Fix the worst framing (gold_bars, drill, saw, wrench, fiddle, magic_dust, tea, peas).

P1 — Kill the anti-patterns

  • Replace “static body + overlay” with object deformation on the big offenders (all ores, most treasure, the drink vessels, furniture, the gem trio, nature's light-cyclers).
  • Replace rigid whole-sprite rotation with articulation (wind, starfish, galaxy, pentacle, bell, tambourine, windmill).

P2 — 12-principles polish pass

  • Add anticipation + overshoot + eased timing + secondary motion to every idle.
  • Convert constant sines into event-driven beats (peck / snip / ring / flash / drip).
  • Couple all shadows to motion; normalize scale/visual-weight to a target band.

Infra & verification

  • Build the shared easing + helper library and a centering/scale utility.
  • Run a loop-seam audit (drive every loop by phase).
  • Re-run the capture + montage/GIF review (this same pipeline) to verify the rebuild.
