Seasonal vector tiles — animation preview

Eighty board tiles drawn entirely with canvas vector paths (no pixel art). Each card below shows one tile in four parts — its four season key frames, its idle loop and its major idle (the rare bold action, isolated on its own loop), and its three forward season→season transitions — the actual tile-drawing code that renders on the board.

Each tile is one parameterized paint() with a per-season parameter set; the transitions are an eased lerp of those params. Two consequences fall out for free: the subject keeps a constant identity across all four seasons (per the project art doc — a cob stays a cob, a pepper stays a bell, a sheep stays a sheep; only colour, frost and pad-dressing change), and every morph starts and ends exactly on the neighbouring season still, so there is no snap at the hand-off into the idle.

Idle is two-tier (WC3-style): a calm baseline plus an occasional bold action — a peck, hop, gust or wing-flare — that on the board fires only every ~12–18s. So you don't have to wait for it, each card isolates the two tiers on their own short loops: Idle = the calm/common motion, Major idle = the big action looped on repeat. What you're looking at

The full board roster — 80 tiles across every category:

  • Trees (6) — oak, birch, cypress, fir, palm, willow. The one category whose foliage shape may change seasonally (evergreens hold + gain snow; deciduous thin/bare), but the trunk keeps identity.
  • Fruit (10) — apple, pear, lemon, blackberry, coconut, golden apple, jackfruit, rambutan, starfruit, melon. One hero fruit, same outline every season; ripeness shows in surface colour, not shape.
  • Grain (5) — corn, wheat, rice, buckwheat, manna. The harvested item only (never a full stalk): the cob / ears / nodding heads / flowering sprig / luminous grains hold one silhouette, green → ripe → drying → frosted.
  • Veg (9) — pepper, mushroom, beet, broccoli, carrot, cucumber, eggplant, squash, turnip.
  • Grass (4) — grass, meadow, spiky, clover (raised tufts/mounds that sway and snow over).
  • Flower (3) — pansy, heather, water lily (its pad reads as water, frozen over in winter).
  • Birds (11) — chicken (with a spring chick), rooster, hen, turkey, goose, wild goose, pheasant, parrot, phoenix (sits in a winter melt-ring), dodo, pig-in-disguise (a pig in a feathered bird costume + a winter scarf).
  • Livestock & mounts (15) — sheep, pig, hog, boar, warthog, alpaca, goat, ram; cow, longhorn, triceratops; horse, donkey, moose (velvet→hard antlers), mammoth. Coat thickens, snow on the back + breath fog in winter.
  • Aquatic, mineral & coin (17) — clam, oyster, mackerel, sardine, kelp, cocoa, octopus, jade, giant pearl (water pads that freeze to ice); stone, iron ore, copper ore, silver, coal, gem, gold and a golden coin (rock & treasure that gain a snow cap + frost).
  • Identity rule. A tile keeps the SAME silhouette in all four seasons — only its surface colour, frost/snow, and the pad's seasonal dressing (spring blossom, autumn fallen leaves, winter snow) change. The subject is always clearly the same thing. (Trees are the lone exception.)
  • Idle vs. major idle. Each idle is two-tier (WC3-style): a calm baseline loop — a slow breath, canopy sway or shimmer — overlaid with an occasional bold action that on the board fires every ~12–18s: a hen pecks then hops with a wing-flap, grain nods in a gust, a flower bobs. The Major idle cell isolates that big action and loops it so you can read it at a glance (a big one like a hop may briefly lift outside the tile box). That baked action is the sole on-board gesture now — the engine keeps vector tiles upright instead of adding its old sprite-rotation sway, so the frame carries all the motion.
  • Transitions. One-shot morphs of colour and shape only (no brightness flash). There is no Winter→Spring morph, so a new run snaps back to Spring; the three cards loop Spring→Summer, Summer→Autumn, Autumn→Winter for review.
