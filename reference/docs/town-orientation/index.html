Town Map Perspective

The town renders its ground dead-overhead but stands the buildings up as flat front-facing facades — two cameras in one scene. Here are three internally-consistent looks, rendered live, to choose a direction from.

Concept review · Status: For decision · 2026-06-21 · src/ui/town/TownScene.ts + src/ui/buildings/*

perspective town scene art direction mockups

What's happening today

The town scene draws on two incompatible cameras:

  • The ground is a flat Phaser tilemap painted from directly overhead — grass / dirt / water on a 40×30 grid (TownScene.create()). A pure plan view.
  • Every building is a straight front-elevation SVG (a flat facade: wall + gable + door, drawn upward from a baseline) stood up as a billboard with setOrigin(0.5, 1).

So the floor implies a camera pointing straight down, while each building implies a camera pointing straight at the wall. That contradiction is the "wrong orientation" you can feel even before you can name it.

TL;DR. Pick one camera and make everything obey it. The cheapest coherent fix — the 2.5D billboard look — keeps every existing building SVG and only polishes the ground; it's the recommended direction. The other two are shown for contrast.

Three coherent looks

Same little hamlet — two cottages, a barn, a bakery, a well, trees, a crossroads and a pond — drawn three ways. These are live SVG, themed to the game's palette; they're massing studies for the perspective, not final art.

1 · True top-down

High art costRedraw all buildings
  • Perfectly consistent — one overhead camera, like a map.
  • Trivial geometry & depth (no sorting); scales to huge zones.
  • Flat and chart-like; loses the warm storybook character.
  • Every building becomes a roof — all current facade art (windows, doors, ovens, smoke) is thrown away.
Recommended

2 · 2.5D billboard (today's art, made coherent)

Low costReuses all SVGs
  • Keeps every existing building SVG as-is — zero per-building redraw.
  • Warm, characterful storybook read; the proven Stardew / classic-JRPG convention.
  • Fix is ground + lighting only: rake the floor slightly, ground each building with a real contact shadow so the standees "sit."
  • A gentle perspective fib (floor tilted, walls head-on) — universally accepted, but not literally correct.

3 · True 3/4 (isometric)

High art costRedraw all buildings
  • Real volume — roof, front and side all visible; premium AAA settlement look.
  • Genuinely consistent: ground and buildings share one projection.
  • Every building must be re-authored in isometric; depth-sorting & footprints get fiddly.
  • Densest to read on a small phone screen; biggest production bill.

Recommendation

Go with Look 2 — the 2.5D billboard. It resolves the perceived mismatch for a fraction of the cost of the other two, because it keeps the ~30 existing building SVGs untouched and only changes how the ground and shadows are drawn. It's also the look players already associate with cozy settlement games.

Reach for isometric (Look 3) only if a premium volumetric look is a deliberate, funded art goal — it's a full re-illustration of every building plus an engine rework. Top-down (Look 1) is rarely worth it here: it's just as much redraw work as iso but throws away the charm that makes the facades appealing in the first place.

If we take the recommended path

The billboard fix is almost entirely in src/ui/town/TownScene.ts; the building SVGs under src/ui/buildings/ stay as they are.

  • Rake the ground. Overlay a soft top-to-bottom light gradient (cooler/darker at the back, warmer at the front) on the tile ground so it reads as a table tilted toward the camera rather than a literal top-down plan.
  • Ground the standees. Buildings are already setOrigin(0.5, 1); give each a firmer elliptical contact shadow pooled at its base (the current shadow is faint, alpha ~0.14) so it visually sits on the floor instead of floating.
  • Consistent light. Pick one sun direction and make sure facade shading + cast shadows agree across buildings, trees and props.
  • Optional depth cue. A faint horizon vignette at the top of the play area sells the rake without touching any sprite.
Per CLAUDE.md. Any of these touches the town render, so run npm run test:visual and justify the golden diffs before opening the PR.

Appendix

Why the facades look "head-on" — one example

src/ui/buildings/bakery.tsx uses viewBox="-54 -90 108 106" with a baseline at y=0 and draws upward: a flat front wall (rect), a front-gable triangle roof (polygon -44,-46 0,-72 44,-46), a door and a window facing the viewer. There is no roof-top plane and no receding side wall — it is a pure orthographic elevation. Every building in the folder follows the same recipe.

About these mockups

The three scenes are generated client-side as inline SVG from one shared hamlet layout, so each look renders the exact same plots, paths, pond, trees and well — only the projection and building massing differ. They're deliberately schematic (palette-accurate, detail-light) to keep the comparison about perspective, not finish.
