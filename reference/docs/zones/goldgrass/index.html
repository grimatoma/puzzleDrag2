Signature mechanic

The capstone of scale and prestige: the grandest layout, the most plots and buildings, civic grandeur (boulevards, ring road, monument) — the agrarian capital the whole journey climbs toward, ringed by the permanent farmland that feeds the realm.

Hazards & boss

  • reuse Locust swarm — Locusts descend on a band of grain tiles and devour them outward each turn.
  • reuse Wildfire — Dry wheat catches and fire spreads to neighbours each turn, destroying resources (fire rules).
  • new Drought — A dry spell cuts grain yields and drains the granary faster until rain returns.
  • new Stampede — A spooked herd charges across the board, trampling a moving band of tiles each turn.

Boss — 🐂 Goldhorn new: The great plains-bull charges every few turns, trampling a full band of tiles in its lane; herd 20 of the right resource into the stockpens (the Stable) before it flattens the harvest.

The craft bar — every rung is reviewed against these

Roads first, buildings front the street

Lay the route network, then place each building beside a route at a fixed setback, facing it. A road is never drawn TO a building's centre — that is the #1 incoherence tell. Roads run between buildings.

The wilderness is the progress bar

Every zone is a clearing pushed into something — forest, ash, ice, dune, fog, void. The receding frontier IS the visible growth. It only ever recedes; cleared ground never re-wilds.

A bounded landmark that levels up in place

One fixed focal hub the buildings ring. It never grows over its neighbours; only its landmark sprite transforms with the town (well → fountain → monument; campfire → forge → magma crown).

Stable, additive growth

Tier N keeps every lot, road and prop of tier N−1 and only APPENDS. A placed building never moves when the town grows. Each upgrade is an earned, legible before/after — a milestone, not a counter tick.

Kill grid-death

Vary lot footprints, jitter placement 5–15%, curve the spine, stagger frontage. A uniform lattice reads as placeholder. Density and ambient life (NPC routines, props, particles) rise each rung.

Distinct identity per rung

Each tier introduces a new landmark, a path-material upgrade (dirt → cobble → stone), and a visible jump in life. The skyline changes. The player should be able to name the era at a glance.

New resources

None — this zone trades in base/board resources only.
