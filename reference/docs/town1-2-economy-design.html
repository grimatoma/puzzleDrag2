Hearthwood Vale — Town 1–2 Economy & Progression

The deep design for the first two towns of the locked Town 1→2→3 slice — the supply-chain-of-boards economy, the floor-3 threshold knobs, the farm→mine "combination", the end-of-Town-2 trickle, and a play-through of each zone — plus a rough outline for Town 3 and beyond.

Overview

This doc specifies the economy and progression for Town 1 (Hearthwood Vale, farm) and Town 2 (Cracked Quarry, the farm+mine "combination"), the heart of the committed slice. It builds within the locked plan on the in-app Direction page, fills the open balance numbers from the Balance baseline (PC2-derived), and records the design decisions taken in review.

TL;DR. The game is a supply chain of boards: each board's turns are bought with the outputs of earlier boards. Town 1 teaches the farm loop; Town 2 makes it literal — a single settlement with both a farm board and a mine board sharing one inventory, where farm-grown food funds mine expeditions. Two chain-length knobs (resource yield + tile-upgrade rate) both floor at 3 and are bought down by global workers; finishing Town 2 retires the mine into a passive trickle that feeds the rest of the kingdom.

The core principle

Progression depth = supply-chain depth. Coins are a soft secondary currency; the real gate on each new board is the basket of goods you must spend to earn turns on it (the expedition). The farm's bread funds the mine's pickaxe; the mine's iron funds the next thing. Every tier feeds the next.

The income-per-run model

Grounded in the live tuned economy (balance.html:14-20):

  • Board & run: 6×6 = 36 cells, min-chain 3, ~10 turns/run (1 turn = 1 chain), avg chain ~5 → ~50 tiles collected/run.
  • Resource income: a chain of N tiles credits floor(progress / divisor) of its resource — a home run nets ~2–3 each of hay / plank / flour (more with bonus-yield buildings, workers, season weighting).
  • Coin income: floor(N · value) per chain (raised ~2× so coins keep pace with sinks).

The chain-length knobs (there are three)

Every chain you drag is read by up to three independent length gates. The design deliberately keeps the first two decoupled in config (income tier-scales; the upgrade gate stays flat) — productionLines.ts:72-101, upgradeThresholds.ts:5.

Why both floor at 3

Min-chain is 3 (chain.ts:59). Flooring both knobs at 3 ties "fully invested" to "one minimum match = one unit / one upgrade" — generous but not broken. At a floor of 1 (today's default) a maxed grass line would give 1 unit per tile, trivialising staples.

Difficulty stays intact (your point, confirmed by PC2 + code). Cheap staples hit the floor with few workers; rare goods start at a high divisor and tier-scale up, so they need many workers and more time to reach the floor. The accelerator is real but self-limiting. PC2 does exactly this — premium lines already floor higher (flowers/mounts floor 3, gems/gold floor 2, productionLines.ts:36,40,44,45).

The expedition cost curve (the spine)

Mine (and later fish) boards aren't free to run: you pack food to buy turns, then play until they run out — soft fail, you keep everything gathered (state.ts:1244-1298, direction.html:32). This basket cost is the master pacing curve of the whole game.

Minimum to depart: 3 turns of food (MIN_EXPEDITION_TURNS, constants.ts:178) — i.e. "3 supplies = standard mine entry" (constants.ts:823). Buildings stretch food: larder/mining_camp +1 turn per ration; the supply-cap ladder (Larder/Pantry/Granary) lets supplies last 20/40/60 days (balance.html:82).

Design lever. Tuning the food→turns rate, the per-run supply cost, and the supply cap is how we set "how long is a mine run" and "how much farming funds one mine trip" — the single most important pacing dial for Town 2.

Coins: faucets & sinks

Coins recede to a soft secondary currency — they bootstrap a new town and lubricate the Market, but tiers and most buildings are resource-only (direction.html:47, balance.html:28).

How you play Town 1 (the play-through)

Fantasy. You arrive at a cold hearth in a forgotten vale; Wren hands you the tongs. You re-light the farm one chain at a time and grow a hamlet into a city — until you can fund a wagon to find what lies beyond.
  1. First chains. Drag through 3+ adjacent matching tiles (8-direction, no reuse). Every threshold-worth of tiles along the chain promotes the end tile a tier and banks floor(chain/divisor) of its resource; the board collapses and refills. That's one of your ~10 turns.
  2. Refine. Send grain to the Mill → flour, flour to the Bakery → bread. Bread is the staple that everything downstream is priced in.
  3. Fill orders & sell. Villagers post orders ("5 bread"); filling them pays coins + bond. Quests and achievements tick quietly in the background (First Steps: first chain → 25 coins), and the Market converts surplus to coins.
  4. Shape the board. Place category-spawner buildings (a Coop puts the whole birds category into the pool) and pick which unlocked categories to run each session. Hire your first worker (−1 tile per resource, and it follows you forever).
  5. Grow the town. Spend farm goods to upgrade the Hearth: Outpost → Hamlet → Village → City. Each rung = more plots + new buildings, nothing else. At Village you unlock the Kitchen (it turns grain into supplies — the food that will power mining).
  6. Finish the zone. Reach City (the top rung) on farm goods alone. City unlocks the wagon: you "raise the wagon", the one clear forcing function, and launch an expedition to discover Town 2. Town 1 stays your hub forever.
Completion test. Town 1 is "done" when you can fund and raise the wagon at City — proving you've learned chain → refine → orders → tier-up → loadout → worker.

Town 2 — Cracked Quarry · the combination PROPOSED shape

The combination is literal. Town 2 is a single settlement with two boards sharing one Town-2 inventory: a farm board (runs on normal turns — your food engine) and a mine board (runs as expeditions). The farm grows the grain → the Kitchen makes supplies → supplies buy mine turns. The supply chain lives entirely inside Town 2's own bucket — no cross-zone transfer needed.
  • Gate: unlocked only when home reaches City — requiresZoneTier:{zone:"home",tier:3} (cartography/data.ts:345), enforced via zoneTierGateReason. Reached by raising the wagon.
  • Mine board: Mine Standard, 10 turns (cartography/data.ts:333). Entry 100 coins.
  • Farm board: PROPOSED add a second board to the quarry node (boards:{ farm, mine }) — the schema already supports it; "multiple boards per zone" is otherwise parked (direction.html:148).
  • Hazards: cave-in, gas vent, mole (cartography/data.ts:335) — urgency the farm never had.

How you play Town 2 (the play-through)

Fantasy. The wagon crests a ridge onto a shattered pit. You raise a working camp beside it, farm the terraces above for food, and spend that food on dangerous descents into the mine — until you've tamed it and can stand against what guards it.
  1. Found & bootstrap. Travel the wagon to the quarry (gated until home was a City) and found it with global coins. Your global workers and tools came with you; the quarry's inventory starts empty.
  2. Run the farm board. Town 2's farm board runs on normal turns — your free, repeatable food engine. Chain grain, build the Kitchen, convert grain → supplies.
  3. Pack a basket & descend. Spend supplies to depart a mine expedition (≥3 turns of food). Each chain spends one turn: stone → block, iron → iron_bar. Cave-ins, gas and moles press you to chain efficiently. Run out of food → the run ends and you keep everything mined.
  4. Build up the mine. Spend block/iron to climb Dig Site → Foundry City, unlocking the Workshop and Forge for metal tools; hire mine workers (global). Use cross-collection when you'd rather clear fast than promote tiles.
  5. Loop the combination. Alternate: farm to refill supplies → descend to mine → refine → grow. This is "the combination" — two economies, one inventory, each feeding the other.
  6. Finish the zone. Build the mine up and beat the ★ capstone boss (reuse Frostmaw) — the dramatic close proving farm + mine mastery (direction.html:34, PARTIAL placement is the build target).
The reward — the trickle. On completion the mine retires to a passive Hinterland: it auto-produces a slow stream of block + iron_bar into your home stockpile with no play required (zones.html:45). Finishing the mine visibly eases everything after — the demo's "idle layer" in miniature. PARKED→pulled · rate & cadence to-sim.

Town 3 — a richer farm & the rats PARTIAL

The slice closes back on the farm with a second, richer hold whose signature lesson is farm-side hazard management mirroring the mine's (direction.html:37). Fed by Town 2's material trickle.

Play-through (sketch). You settle a lusher farm and your barns fill — which draws rats (they spawn once grain/grass inventory is high and eat adjacent crops each turn, features/farm/rats.ts). You learn to manage them: chain rats directly to clear them, build the Ratcatcher (free moves for rats) and the Master Ratcatcher (collect rats together with grass — tile-combine). You also open a deeper crop economy and the town's own specialization. Complete it by taming the rat hazard while growing the richer-farm ladder.
