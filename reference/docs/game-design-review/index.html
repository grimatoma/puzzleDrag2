Full design review · 2026-07

puzzleDrag2 — a complete design pass

Every view, layout, interaction, animation, and critical journey — read from the live code in src/ (not the reference/ docs), with the running game screenshotted across ~85 states at both a phone (390×844) and a desktop (1280×1024) viewport, and judged mobile-first through a frontend-design lens. Findings and concrete proposals, organized into the tabs at left.

The short version. This is a genuinely charming, deep game with a excellent signature interaction and a strong storybook art identity. The prior color/contrast pass landed well — the parchment UI now has a real contrast ladder. The work that's left is not more color. It is four things: (1) the game has no wide-screen layout — every DOM screen is an unadapted phone column stranded in empty parchment on desktop/web; (2) the town's world-overlays and its central Farm plot look unfinished next to the rich building art; (3) a class of legibility/polish papercuts (label truncation, redundant reward rows, grass "confetti" noise, dead desktop space); and (4) the reward/feedback and motion languages don't yet form one escalating ladder from the board out into the chrome. None require a rebuild — they're targeted, high-leverage fixes.

§How this was evaluated

  • Source of truth = the code. Assessments were formed from src/, the three Vite entries, and the running game — never from reference/ docs.
  • Screenshots of the real game. All ~85 visual scenarios (from src/visualTesting/matrix.ts) were rendered against the running dev server at a phone and a desktop viewport. Shots referenced below live in ./shots/.
  • Mobile-first. The primary target is a phone in portrait; density, tap targets, reach and truncation are judged there first. Desktop shots reveal full layouts.
  • Beyond color. A prior review already rebuilt the token contrast ladder, accent system, season accents and bundled the display font (see src/tokens.css). This pass deliberately pushes past that into layout, IA, motion/feel, per-view UX, and whether the player understands what is happening.
  • Method. Four subsystem maps (views/IA, critical journeys, design system, interaction/animation) grounded a fan-out of focused reviewers — one per surface and per cross-cutting lens — each citing the screenshot and the file:line behind every finding.

§What's genuinely strong — protect these

  • The chain-drag is a great signature interaction. The glowing path with node dots and halo, the star-burst upgrade previews, chain-length-scaled screen-shake + radial flash, escalating haptics and audio pitch, squash-stretch landing, and the BONUS→DOUBLE→TRIPLE→FRENZY combo panel all combine into a satisfying, legible harvest. The held-chain state (see the Board tab) reads beautifully. This is the heart of the game and it works.
  • The town & building art is rich and characterful. The painterly settlement — bakery, mill, portal, market stalls, houses, lamps, benches, river with a boat — genuinely feels like a place that grows across tiers.
  • The World Map is the best-realized screen. A cohesive parchment atlas with themed zone nodes, solid-vs-dashed roads, a clean detail panel and a real legend. It nails the storybook identity and it actually uses the widescreen space. It is the internal benchmark for every other screen.
  • Quest / tile / research cards are legible. Category tags, have/need progress bars, reward chips, and clear claim states make goals scannable.
  • The season system is ambitious and mostly reads. Distinct field gradients and per-season accents give each of the four seasons a felt identity.

§The big themes

Seven patterns recur across the whole game. Every specific finding below rolls up into one of these. Detail lives in the Cross-cutting tab; sequencing in the Roadmap.

T1Systems exist but are unreachable, unnamed, or scattered
A large share of built content has no player-facing door. Seven full-screen systems (Orders, Castle, Bosses, Portal, Decorations, Tiles, Chronicle) are reachable only via the Debug menu; Boons hides behind a story-flag-gated floating button; Charter is a drill-down under a hub with no entry. Where systems are reachable, the taxonomy misleads ("Townsfolk" = only Workers; "Almanac" names three different things; the menu says "Hearthlands" while everything else says "Hearthwood Vale") and related goal systems (Daily, Orders, Achievements) sit on three unconnected surfaces. The 6-tab bottom nav can't scale and has no "More"/hub pattern, so 9+ views are homeless.
T2Rewards & progress are illegible — you can't see what you earned or what a thing is worth
The economy runs but the payoff is hidden at every junction. XP is invisible in the moment and absent from the run summary; level-up celebrates a bare number with no "here's what unlocked." Crafting rows never show what a craft produces or its coin yield. Order rewards render as floating berry-icon bubbles instead of legible coin payouts. Boss victory reward is the faintest text on the modal. Fractional harvest progress silently vanishes when you stop chaining. The board never states a season goal, only a decrementing turn budget.
T3Two identities fight: warm painterly parchment vs. flat/generic mobile-game chrome
The brand is warm parchment + painterly canvas art, but the app keeps breaking it. Board tiles sit on saturated per-category "candy" squares that clash and override the seasonal read; two icon families (flat vector vs. painterly canvas-baked, plus gloss/3D thumbnails) render side by side; the boss modal is a generic dark-scrim popup; story variants use a bespoke serif and hardcoded palette divorced from tokens; locked tools render as flat gray icons that read as broken art; the town grass is buried under dense multicolored flower speckle that reads as pixel noise.
T4Layout is not truly responsive — mobile is starved and desktop wastes space
The play surface loses on both viewports. On desktop the square board floats in a stretched-wide portrait column with ~40–45% dead parchment; list views render as narrow centered columns. On mobile a tall chrome stack + fixed-height action panel + season strip push the board into the lower ~55% (below the fold); the trophy grid truncates every title; inventory wastes width; crafting hides inputs/Craft behind a tap; the map detail panel is capped so CTAs fall below a fold; headers truncate ("The Hearthwo…"). The 44px tap-target token is defined but never applied.
T5Feedback & motion are incoherent and accessibility-unsafe
Reduced-motion is honored for idle sway but not for the vestibular-heavy effects — screen-shake, radial flash, particle bursts, collapse/refill — on both board and town, and tokens.css documents a motion policy that contradicts index.css. Motion-duration tokens are defined but consumed nowhere; cinematic holds are unrelated magic numbers; the run summary (the season climax) has zero motion. Feedback is missing where it matters: a too-short chain release gives zero feedback; tap tools fire blind; an active boss leaves no on-board reminder; a 1500ms warm-up makes the first board look frozen.
T6Placeholder / empty / locked / mislabeled states leak into the finished product
States that should degrade gracefully read as broken: zero-count inventory items shown at full prominence; the currently-researched tile labeled "Locked"; category tabs with no text labels (mismatched keys); boss tributes as raw snake_case; a wall of 24 identical "???" cells; trophy cards that say "Done" while progress reads 12/100; empty "Plot" ghost boxes; dead-end CTAs that point at "Build the Bakery in town" with no route there; an onboarding spotlight aimed at an off-screen (−9999px) element and the wrong nav tab.
T7Modal, CTA, and status vocabulary are inconsistent across the shell
The chrome lacks a single spec: two competing modal shells (titled+divider, no-X vs. centered title with top-right X); a leave-board confirm that warns of loss without saying what's lost; inverted CTA emphasis on the Town (pale primary "Enter", loud green secondary "Build") plus two floating CTAs; status words ("ready", "No station", "Village", "Locked", "Done", "violated") carried by bare text with no visual differentiation; NPC bubbles and Market News as generic portrait-less top-center toasts; a Menu of 9–10 equal-weight buttons in the hardest-to-reach corner.

Core surface · 01

The Board — the puzzle

The 6×6 drag-to-chain board is the heart of the game and its best-executed interaction. The feedback during a chain is genuinely excellent; the problems are in how the board is framed (layout on both viewports, the season strip, the stockpile) and in the feedback that's missing at the edges (failed chains, tap-tools, hazards, bosses, reduced-motion).

What works — protect it.
  • The chain-in-progress panel is superb: a live fill bar, the inline "UPGRADE TO Pheasant 2/6 → +1" strip, the escalating BONUS!/DOUBLE!/TRIPLE!/FRENZY! tier chips (puzzleBoard.tsx:149), and the "N more to collect" header when short. You always know what a chain is worth and how close the next reward is.
  • The build-up feedback ladder is layered and tuned: rising per-tile audio pitch (dragFeedback.ts:12), a one-shot "chainReady" cue + stronger buzz at the collectable threshold, escalating haptics, and the animated growing path + ring-burst + tier-scaled star previews (GameScene.ts:1559).
  • The armed-tool banner is clear and safe (name, plain-language effect, "TAP A TILE", big DISARM). Dead boards auto-reshuffle instead of soft-locking (GameScene.ts:1078). The season-tinted tile art reads instantly at a glance.

§Layout & framing

S1LDesktop board is a blown-up phone layout — a small square stranded in ~40% dead parchment
Screensboard-farm-idle, board-mine-idle, board-fish-idle-high-tide (desktop) EvidenceThe board grid is minmax(360px,44%) minmax(0,1fr) with the board a square centered in its cell (puzzleBoard.tsx BOARD_LAYOUT_CSS ~1749; game/layout.ts:54,61), so a wide short column yields a small centered square with dead margins on every side. DiagnosisThe board is the signature object, yet on desktop it does not dominate — it's small and off-center while a thin rail of secondary chrome (stockpile 0-rows, locked tools) competes. Classic "portrait blown up to desktop": nothing was re-composed for the wide aspect, so hierarchy inverts. ProposalGive desktop a purpose-built composition: center the board in the full width, cap its size and let it grow toward the ceiling, and dock the rail (tools + stockpile) as a fixed-width sidebar or float the panel/tools as compact overlays anchored to the board corners. Move the tool grid into the dead bottom-right rather than stacking it in the narrow rail.
S1MSeason-strip labels overprint the season art and the walking wagon — unreadable, worst on fishing
Screensboard-fish-idle-high-tide, board-farm-idle, board-boss-weather EvidenceSeason zones are proportional to turn-count, not equal quarters (seasonStripScene.ts:306), and each full label is centered in its (often narrow) zone with no width-fit (buildLabels ~776); labels and the wagon sprite share the same bottom band (buildWagon:821). On a 390px phone the numeral panel eats 56px, leaving ~300px for four words — which collide with the icons and the wagon. DiagnosisThe strip is the player's only readout of "what season / how many turns left," and it's illegible in exactly the state (fishing, narrow) where they look. Text-on-text-on-sprite is a hard readability failure, not polish. ProposalStop drawing four full words. Show only the current season name once (large, in the numeral panel) plus tick marks — or abbreviate to SPR/SUM/AUT/WIN, elide any label whose zone is narrower than its text, and move the wagon to its own track so sprite and text never share pixels. On fish, give the tide chip its own row.
S2MSTOCKPILE panel burns prime top real-estate on mostly-empty "0/greyed" rows
Screensboard-farm-idle, board-mine-idle, board-fish-idle-high-tide EvidenceIdle lists every reachable resource up to 12, rendering empties at 0.55 opacity (puzzleBoard.tsx IdleView ~233); the panel is a fixed 148px regardless of content (~768). Fish idle shows "0/0 KINDS" — a full panel saying nothing; mine shows "1/3 KINDS" with 2 greyed. DiagnosisThe densest state drove the panel height, so an empty stockpile reserves 148px of the most valuable band to say nothing; on mobile this pushes the board down, on desktop it pads the rail. The panel is loud where it should be quiet. ProposalCollapse empties — owned first, hide 0-count kinds behind a "+N more" chip or a muted "not yet gathered" footer. When a biome has 0 kinds, replace the card with a one-line hint ("Chain fish to start your catch") and let the panel shrink so the board rises. Keep the fixed height only for the chain/tool states that need it.
S2MTools rack presents greyed locked/empty tools as first-class tiles
Screensboard-farm-idle, board-mine-idle EvidencevisiblePuzzleTools returns any reachable-or-owned tool regardless of count (puzzleToolFilter.ts:113); count-0 tiles render at grayscale(0.85), opacity 0.55 (ToolTile ~887). Desktop shows ~7 owned tools then a block of dead grey tiles (rake 0, axe 0, sickle 0…); mobile shows empty dashed hotbar slots. DiagnosisGreyed "0" tiles read as broken/disabled affordances in the primary action rack — the player can't tell "not crafted yet" from "out of charges" from "wrong biome." A wall of dead tiles dilutes the few live tools and adds load every board entry. ProposalSeparate "owned & usable" from "craftable but unowned." Show only count>0 tools (plus armed) in the rack/hotbar; move zero-count reachable tools into a subdued "Craft more" shelf with a distinct locked treatment. Drop the empty dashed hotbar slots on mobile.
S2MHazard atmosphere is invisible — "fire & rats" looks identical to idle
Screensboard-farm-fire-rats vs board-farm-idle EvidenceThe fire/rats shot is visually indistinguishable from idle — no flames, smoke, gnawed tiles, rat sprites, tint, vignette or banner (only a bee is added). The GameScene hazard atmosphere (~529) produces no legible signal in the capture. DiagnosisHazards are a core seasonal threat, but if the board doesn't visibly change the player has no idea one is present or which tiles are threatened — "a hazard is attacking your board" fails legibility-of-state. (Even the tide state at least gets a chip.) ProposalGive active hazards an unmistakable board signal: a colored edge glow/vignette on the frame (reuse the armed-pulse), a hazard chip in the rail head naming it + turns-to-resolve, and per-tile markers (smoke/flame/gnaw) on affected tiles. Also verify the fire/rats atmosphere renders at all — the shot suggests it's absent or far too subtle.

§Feel & feedback

S2SReleasing a too-short chain gives ZERO feedback — the path silently vanishes
Screensboard-farm-chain-2-short EvidenceA sub-min release calls clearPath(true) with no sound, shake, or animation (GameScene.ts:1730). An "error" descending-buzz SFX is defined (audio/index.ts:83) but play('error') is called nowhere in src. DiagnosisGame feel demands a proportional response to every input. A committed drag that fails the length gate produces nothing, so a beginner can't tell whether the game ignored them or they just need one more tile. Next to the lavish success juice, total silence on failure reads as a bug. ProposalOn the invalid branch, fire the existing "error" SFX at low volume, a short haptic (vibrate(20)), and a quick shrink+fade of the dropped path (alpha→0, scale→0.9 over ~140ms) plus a one-shot red flash. Keep it brief so near-misses don't nag.
S2MTap-target tools (bomb, sickle) show no preview of what they'll hit — you spend a scarce charge blind
Screensboard-farm-tool-bomb, board-farm-tool-sickle EvidenceThe banner says "destroys a 3×3 area" / "harvest that entire row" but the board shows no highlight. Bomb has no dim strategy → clearDimming() (GameScene.ts:1767); sickle's "type_multi" never highlights the row. Firing is immediate on tap (~1447) with no hover/confirm. DiagnosisTools are consumable and scarce (bomb ×3). An area/line effect with no aim preview means you can't see the footprint before spending the charge — a mis-tap wastes an irreversible resource. Feedback must precede commitment for destructive area actions. ProposalOn pointer-move while armed, highlight the exact affected cells (glowing 3×3 for bomb, full-row band for sickle/rake). Add a brief press-and-hold-to-aim (release to fire) or two-tap confirm for area tools so the preview is actually seen.
S2MAn active boss leaves no persistent on-board reminder; its raised min-chain surfaces only mid-drag
Screensboard-boss-active, board-boss-minimized EvidenceBoth render as a plain stockpile board with no objective, timer, or min-chain banner. Boss UI is a dismissable modal (features/boss/index.tsx:9); the scene consumes boss.minChain only inside _effectiveMinChain (GameScene.ts:598) and only surfaces via the chain panel's "N more" during a drag. DiagnosisOnce the modal is dismissed, the player fights a boss with no visible objective, no progress toward the target, and no reminder that chains now need 5+ — they discover the raised gate by failing a drag (which is itself silent, per the finding above). A signature moment vanishes from the play surface. ProposalAdd a compact persistent boss banner in/above the action panel: portrait, name, progress bar (boss.progress/targetCount already computed), and a "Chains need N+" pill when min-chain is raised. Tint the idle panel with the flame accent so the board signals "boss active" at rest.
S2SReduced-motion gates idle sway but NOT screen-shake, radial flash, particles, or collapse
Screensboard-farm-chain-7  ·  (see also the Accessibility & Motion cross-cutting lenses) Evidence_motionEnabled() checks prefers-reduced-motion but gates only ambient tile sway (GameScene.ts:2149). _shake calls cameras.main.shake unconditionally (485, fired on every 3+ chain), radialFlash always fires (2052), emitCollectParticles always explodes (572), and _dur() is an identity passthrough. DiagnosisScreen shake and full-screen flashes are exactly the effects that trigger vestibular discomfort — far more than a swaying tile. Gating only the least-harmful motion inverts the accessibility priority and makes reduced-motion effectively non-functional for the signature interaction. ProposalRoute shake, flash, upgrade-burst, and particle counts through one reduced-motion check. When on: skip the camera shake, replace the flash with a brief static tint pulse, cut particles to a token few — keeping the audio/haptic ladder so success still feels rewarded.

§Also noted (S3 / polish)

  • Winter/mine tiles lose type-identity (S3) — snow/grey wash flattens wheat/carrot/apple/tree into similar mounds (board-season-winter) and the first mine columns blur into grey lumps; a match game needs instant silhouette parsing. Preserve each crop's silhouette through the overlay; push hue/shape separation between ore tiers. Per-tile art pass.
  • Tide/mine status vocabulary is inconsistent (S3) — fish gets a tiny top-right "High Tide ·3" pill with an unlabeled countdown; mine gets no board-state chip at all. Standardize one rail-head status-chip slot across all three biomes and label the countdown ("flips in 3").
  • Mobile board sits below a tall chrome stack (S3) — railhead → tools → 148px stockpile → board (begins ~45% down). Collapse the idle stockpile and pin a thin hotbar so the board rises above the fold.
  • Fertilizer has no armed state (S3) — unlike bomb/sickle/rake it shows no banner; the biased-spawn payoff is diffuse and delayed, so it feels like it did nothing. Add a persistent "Fertilizer active" chip + a one-shot sparkle on the biased spawns.
  • 1500ms idle-motion warm-up (S3) — the first-seen board looks frozen before it "comes alive" (ANIM_STARTUP_DELAY_MS). Shorten to ~400–600ms or ramp amplitude 0→1 so it's subtly alive immediately.
  • Auto-reshuffle notice is a tiny transient float (S3) — the same micro-float used for "+N resource," so players miss why the board rearranged. Promote to a ~1.2s centered banner shown before the shuffle spin.
  • Overloaded dim signal (POLISH) — "can't select" and "legal but low-value" both use 0.35 alpha, so useful tool targets read as disabled. Use a lighter dim (~0.6) for suboptimal, or invert to a positive glow on high-value targets.

Core surface · 02

The Town — the hub & first impression

The town is the hub and the first surface after onboarding. The building art and the sense of a settlement that grows are real strengths — but a cluster of compounding issues (a dirt-pit board bed, confetti grass, debug-looking founding boxes, inverted CTAs) make the primary gameplay entry read as unfinished. This is the screen with the highest ratio of "cheap fix → big first-impression win."

What works. The vector town props (cottages, windmill, stalls, lamps, fountain, benches) are charming and depth-sorted, and the town visibly densifies fresh→built-out→city (TownScene.rebuildBuildingsAndPlots ~749) — the "growing settlement" promise reads. Per-biome identity is distinct (farm = warm grass, mine = desert/scrub, harbor = water-edged pier), and board-entry pills carry the correct biome accent on their border (Town.tsx:441).
S1MThe Farm/Harbor board bed — the primary gameplay entry — renders as a flat empty dirt pit / water rectangle
Screenstown-home-fresh, town-home-built-out, town-harbor-settlement EvidenceTownScene.drawBoards fills the whole footprint with one ground tile — DIRT for farm/mine, WATER for fish (TownScene.ts:690) — then draws only a 2px border + a 68px label plate. No crop rows, furrows, tools or produce on the farm bed (mine at least scatters 4 ore boulders; farm/fish get nothing). DiagnosisThe board is the signature interaction, yet its town entry looks like unbuilt land — a dirt pit and a puddle, with less visual life than a decorative plot. On the fresh screen it competes with the empty "Plot" ghosts and loses. This undercuts the invitation to play. ProposalDress each board bed as a living mini-scene: tile 3–4 furrow rows with a few seasonal crop sprites + a scarecrow/basket on the farm (reuse the seasonal tile catalog); add ripple/foam + a moored boat on the harbor; keep the mine's boulders + a cart/pickaxe. Add a subtle idle shimmer or a pulsing "tap to enter" halo so the board out-reads the plots.
S2SDense multicolored grass speckle is visual noise from TWO compounding random-flower systems
Screensshell-town-fresh, town-home-fresh, town-home-built-out EvidenceTwo independent scatterers stack: (1) proceduralGround paints per-pixel specks from a 4-colour palette wherever hash>0.993 (proceduralGround.ts:291); (2) TownScene.scatterGroundDetail stamps GRASS_FLOWER tiles on 4% of grass cells (TownScene.ts:561). DiagnosisAt default zoom the specks are sub-tile confetti with no legible shape — they read as JPEG noise / dead pixels, not meadow flowers, and add chromatic clutter that fights the readable buildings and board. Boldness is spent everywhere, so nothing calms the eye — the loudest ambient texture is the wrong loud thing. ProposalPick ONE flower system. Drop the per-pixel proceduralGround specks (noisiest), lower scatterGroundDetail from 0.04 to ~0.012, and cluster flowers (place one only if a neighbour also rolled one) so they read as intentional patches. Desaturate the palette ~20%.
S2MEmpty "Plot" ghosts read as broken/placeholder UI — and are inert (non-tappable) outside build mode
Screenstown-home-fresh, town-build-picker-ready, town-mine-settlement EvidenceFloating rounded rects labeled "Plot" in white on a 14%-black fill (TownScene.ts:770); the interactive hit-zone is only added if (isPlacing) (~786), so a tap outside build mode does nothing. DiagnosisA translucent dark box labeled "Plot" over grass looks like a debug rect or a loading placeholder, not "buildable land." Worse, it looks like a button but is dead unless you're already in build mode — a silent no-op that teaches the town is unresponsive. ProposalStyle plots as in-world affordances (light dashed picket/rope border, faint sunlit-grass fill, a small "For Build" signpost or foundation stones — no dark overlay). Make them tappable at all times; a tap outside build mode opens the BuildPicker pre-targeted to that plot.
S2MFounding a settlement stacks 3–4 dark floating boxes that read as a debug overlay
Screenstown-found-biome-picker, town-unfounded-blocked EvidenceStacked at screen-center: a black "…isn't yours yet" pill, a brown "Found this settlement · 12 Plank · 10 Bread Loaf · 8 Hay Bundle" sign, and a red gate pill — three separately-positioned divs, each with its own background (FoundSettlementBanner, Town.tsx:114). DiagnosisThree differently-coloured dark boxes floating over the map, none anchored, read as stacked debug toasts. The message (name, cost, blocker) is fragmented across three surfaces with three visual languages — the signature "found a new town" moment feels like an error state. ProposalCollapse into ONE carved-wood signpost card: title ("Claim Wild Orchard"), a cost row using RequirementChips (green owned / amber short), and a single inline lock line with the blocker + icon — one background, one border, planted on a post graphic so it reads as an in-world sign.
S2SCTA emphasis is inverted — the core action ("Enter") is pale; the sink ("Build") is the loud green one
Screenstown-home-fresh, town-home-built-out, town-harbor-settlement Evidence"Enter Farm Field" is cream (#fbf4e2, biome border — Town.tsx:452); "Build · N/M plots" is a saturated green gradient (#6db83f→#4f8c2a, :413). Build is the visually dominant CTA in every state. DiagnosisEntering the board is the core loop (you can't build without first earning on the board); building is the spend side-action. Making Build the single loudest thing mis-points new players toward the sink before the faucet — and two co-equal pills read as a paired choice, not a primary/secondary relationship. ProposalSwap emphasis: give "Enter Farm Field" the confident filled accent (ideally the board's biome colour), demote "Build" to a soft/outline secondary. Or keep Build green only when plots are free and affordable, else mute it so it stops shouting when the player can't act.
S2SHarbor HUD collides — settlement name overlaps the Tide pill and coins on mobile
Screenstown-harbor-settlement Evidence"Saltspray Ha…" collides with a "High Tide ·3" pill overlapping the "12,500" coins pill.
