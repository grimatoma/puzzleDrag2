Proposals · Surface 05

Story & big moments — fully-defined proposals

The narrative bones are unusually good. The work here is to make the peaks — a level-up, a season's climax, a boss standing in your path — land with the weight the writing already earns. Each proposal pairs the game as it is now with a rendered mock of what to build and a concrete file:line spec.

What already works — keep it. The three-way story split is genuinely smart: heavy beats get a center-stage modal, one-liners get a bottom bar, the finale keeps its gilded card (Modals.tsx:534–542). And the run-summary is strong content — best-moment hero, a resource tally, bond deltas, supplies consumed, all in one screen (runSummary/index.tsx). The fixes below don't rebuild any of that. They (S1) put a reward under the level-up numeral, (S2) give the season's climax motion and promote a story beat out of the receipt, (S3) make the Chronicle an actual timeline, (S4) unify the narrative onto one bundled-Fraunces surface, and (S5) reskin the boss popup out of F2P-interstitial territory. None is a rewrite.

S1S2Effort S–M Top priority #2 partner
Put a reward under the level-up numeral — say what actually unlocked

A level-up is one of the game's few earned peaks, and it fires a full-bleed cinematic that shows only the number. No tier name, no reward, no "what's new" — the moment that should sell progression is the emptiest screen in the game. It holds for a fixed 1600ms and is pointer-events-none, so it can't be tapped through or into. LevelUpCinematic.tsx:5 (HOLD_MS=1600), :36 (pointer-events-none), :44–49 (the lone numeral). The reward data already exists — ALMANAC_TIERS carries a name + description + reward for every level (features/almanac/data.ts:14).

NOWa bare numeral
PROPOSEDnumeral + tier name + reward
Options — how interactive should the peak be
A · Timed, but tappableRecommended
Keep the auto-dismiss (1600ms with a reward, ~1100ms without) but drop pointer-events-none so a tap dismisses early, and make the reward line a link into the Almanac. Pro: respects the player's time, still ambient for those who don't touch it. Con: one extra tap-target to wire and test.
B · Hold until dismissed
On the levels that unlock something real, hold with a "Continue / Open Almanac" button instead of a timer. Pro: guarantees the reward is seen. Con: interrupts flow mid-run; reserve it for milestone tiers (2 / 5 / 10) rather than every level.
Implementation
  • In src/ui/LevelUpCinematic.tsx:44, after the numeral, look up ALMANAC_TIERS.find(t => t.level === shown.level) from features/almanac/data.ts:14 and render tier.name + a compact reward badge derived from tier.reward (tools / structural / coins → one human label).
  • Make HOLD_MS conditional (LevelUpCinematic.tsx:5): ~1600ms when a tier line renders, ~1100ms when none does.
  • Drop pointer-events-none (:36) for Option A; add an onPointerDown that clears shown early, and route the reward line to SET_VIEW: "almanac".
  • Fix the aria-live region to announce the tier, not the bare digit (see S7).

Partners with the report's level-up priority — this is the "make the reward legible" half; the almanac-claim flow is the other.

S2S2Effort M
Stage the run-summary reveal — and lift a triggered beat out of the receipt

This is the season's climax and it's the stillest screen in the game: a ParchmentDialog that paints everything at once with zero motion. And when a story beat fires during the run, it's demoted to a row of small gold pills under a "Story beats" label near the bottom — the same visual weight as "Supplies consumed" — so the narrative payoff reads as a line-item on a receipt. runSummary/index.tsx renders top-to-bottom with no animation; BeatsBlock ~:120–133 is the buried pill row; ParchmentDialog.Body ~:183.

NOW — real rendermobile · everything at once
PROPOSEDstaged · beat elevated
Implementation

S3S2Effort M
Make the Chronicle a real timeline — order by when it happened, not by authoring

The Chronicle is a keepsake of your journey, but it has no chronology. It filters STORY_BEATS by their completion flag and renders them in authoring order, stamping each with a static Act {beat.act || 1} label — so a fresh save's log is a wall of rows that all read "ACT 1", in an order the player never experienced. Meanwhile a timestamped choiceLog ({ beatId, choiceId, ts }) is recorded on every pick and read nowhere here — the Charter screen already sorts it by ts, so the pattern exists. chronicle/index.tsx:14 (flag filter, authoring order), :50 (dateStr = `Act ${beat.act || 1}`); log written at features/story/slice.ts:119, seeded story.ts:22; sorted precedent at features/charter/index.tsx:329.

NOWevery row says "ACT 1"
PROPOSEDseason markers · played order
Options — what the grouping key is
A · Year · Season markersRecommended
Sort by ts; header each group with the in-world "Year N · Season" the beat was completed in (derivable from the run/season counter). Pro: reads as a diary, matches the game's seasonal clock. Con: needs the completion season stamped on the log entry (small schema add).
B · Act chapters, ts within
Keep Act I/II/III chapter headers but order beats within each act by ts, and collapse repeated act labels. Pro: zero schema change — uses beat.act + the existing log. Con: still no real dates; early game stays one long "Act I".
Implementation
  • In chronicle/index.tsx:14, build the list from story.choiceLog (sorted by ts) joined to STORY_BEATS by beatId, instead of filtering beats by flag in authoring order — mirror features/charter/index.tsx:329.
  • Replace dateStr = `Act ${beat.act || 1}` (chronicle/index.tsx:50) with a group header; render one header per contiguous group and drop the per-row act label.
  • For Option A, stamp season/year onto each choiceLog entry at write time (features/story/slice.ts:119) so the marker is exact rather than inferred.
S4S2Effort M
One narrative surface — bundle Fraunces into the story, retire the hardcoded palette

The three story variants share a bespoke serif and a hand-tuned color object that live entirely outside the token system. STORY_SERIF is an OS-fallback stack ("Iowan Old Style" → Palatino → Georgia) — not the Fraunces the rest of the game bundles and uses for display type — so story text is set in whatever serif the device happens to have. And because the modal renders on dark iron while the Chronicle renders on warm parchment (hl-card), the same beat reads in two different palettes and two different faces depending on where you meet it. Modals.tsx:79 (STORY_SERIF), :82–94 (hardcoded SC color object), StoryStagePanel:340 (dark-iron bg). Fraunces is bundled at main.tsx:5–6 and led by --font-display (tokens.css:203).

NOWsame beat, two skins
PROPOSEDone story token block
Implementation
  • Set STORY_SERIF = "var(--font-display)" (Modals.tsx:79) so story type uses the already-bundled Fraunces (main.tsx:5).
  • Add a --story-stage / --story-parchment / --story-gold / --story-narration block to src/tokens.css and replace the hardcoded SC object (Modals.tsx:82–94) with those vars.
  • Have the Chronicle card (chronicle/index.tsx:61) consume the same --story-* tokens so a beat looks identical wherever it appears.
S5S2Effort M–L Cross-cutting T3
Reskin the boss modal into the storybook — and promote the reward to a gold pill

The boss encounter is the game's antagonist moment, and its popup reads like a free-to-play interstitial: a hard black/70 scrim, a flat tan card with a bright orange 4px frame and orange gradient CTA, and a cute cartoon-blob portrait that undercuts the "frozen titan" the copy is selling. The reward — the thing the player is weighing "accept" against — is the faintest text on the card (white/50, buried between the goal panel and the button). features/boss/index.tsx:44–52 (black/70 scrim + --flame-frame border), :65 (emoji-blob fallback), :131–134 (faint reward line), :138–148 (orange-gradient CTA).

NOW — real rendermobile · F2P interstitial
PROPOSEDvellum · season-tinted · reward pill
Implementation
  • Swap the modal chrome in features/boss/index.tsx:44–52: darker frost-tinted scrim, a vellum (--story-parchment) card, and a frame color driven by the boss's season rather than the fixed --flame-frame.
  • Promote the reward: move the features/boss/index.tsx:131–134 line into a gold Pill rendered next to the "Accept the challenge" button (:138).
  • Replace the emoji fallback (:65) with a shaded painterly placeholder (the same treatment as StoryPortrait) so bosses without art still read as menacing; darken the BossCinematic vignette to match (src/ui/BossCinematic.tsx).
  • Ensure the card sits on an opaque scrim so town CTAs can't bleed through (shared fix with S6).
S6S3Effort S each
Also folded in — small narrative-frame fixes

Low-effort corrections found while grounding the above. Each is a localized change, not a redesign.

  • "Board paused while we talk." shows where there is no board — the bottom story bar always prints that caption and uses a light rgba(0,0,0,0.28) scrim, so on the Town (no board) it describes something that isn't there and the town's CTAs bleed through the dim (Modals.tsx:443, scrim at :409). Make the caption context-aware (hide it off-board) and deepen/opaque the scrim so nothing underneath is tappable.
  • Boss intro art undercuts the menace — the cinematic and modal show a friendly blob. Short term: darken the scene behind the reveal and add a frost/season vignette so even the cartoon reads as a threat (src/ui/BossCinematic.tsx); long term is the painterly portrait in S5.
  • Chronicle bakes speaker names into prose — entries fall back to beat.body || beat.lines[0].text, so any line that names its speaker inline reads awkwardly out of dialogue context (chronicle/index.tsx:62). Prefer an authored beat.summary/body written as narration for the log.
  • The win finale buries "sandbox continues" in a parenthetical — "…there is more of the old kingdom still to find. (Sandbox mode continues.)" (src/story.ts:305). Lift it into a proper "Keep playing" affordance on the finale card so players know the game doesn't end.
  • Multi-line beats gate the footer with no progress dots — a long beat shows a "Next line" cue but no n-of-m indicator, so the reader can't tell how much dialogue remains (Modals.tsx:546–553). Add small step dots when canStepDialogue is true.
S7NEWfrom this pass
New findings surfaced while grounding these mockups

Not in the original report; found reading the story/level-up/boss code for this pass. Verify against the live build before scheduling.

  • Level-up unlock copy is hardcoded in the reducer, divorced from ALMANAC_TIERS — the level-up bubble writes "Level 2! Mine biome unlocked…" and otherwise "Level N! New things await." inline in the chain reducer (src/state.ts:587–591), while the real per-level reward names live in ALMANAC_TIERS (features/almanac/data.ts:14). Two out-of-sync sources of "what leveling gives you." S1 should feed both the cinematic and this bubble from the tier table so they can't drift.
  • Tier rewards require a manual claim, but nothing signals one is waiting — claimAlmanacTier only grants a reward when the player opens the Almanac and claims it (features/almanac/data.ts:111), yet the level-up moment gives zero pointer to go do so. Rewards can silently pile up unclaimed. The S1 cinematic is the natural nudge.
  • The level-up aria-live announces a bare digit — the region is aria-live="polite" but its only content is the numeral (LevelUpCinematic.tsx:36,45), so a screen-reader user hears "5" with no context. Announcing "Level 5 — Master Harvester unlocked" rides for free on the S1 data lookup.
