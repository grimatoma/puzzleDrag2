Event log

How to play

  • Drag a chain across touching tiles (8 directions). The 1st tile fixes family A; the 2nd touching tile of a different family fixes B and locks the pair.
  • From there each next tile must touch the last and be the opposite family, so the chain reads A,B,A,B,… — only the two locked families are eligible.
  • Release a braid of 4 or more to weave a Blend: pts = length + (length−3)². Longer braids pay off much faster. Then the chained tiles clear, columns cascade down, and the board refills from the top.

Why it is ours: It changes only the connection grammar — same drag gesture, same board, same refill — but rewards a totally different kind of board-reading (interleaved patterns) than "find a blob of identical tiles". A safe, shippable twist that adds depth without a second puzzle verb.
