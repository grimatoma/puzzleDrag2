Deep Layers

The mine gains a third dimension. Each cell is a STACK — clear the top ore and you dig down to reveal the richer (and more dangerous) layer beneath. Stone over iron over gem over gold… but water floods and gas pockets wait in the deep. Excavate, do not just match.

Event log

How to play

  • Drag across 3+ touching tiles showing the same ore (8 directions). Release to excavate that vein.
  • There is no refill from above. Each cleared cell digs one layer deeper in place — revealing the richer ore beneath (or a hazard). The corner number is its current depth.
  • Score per tile scales with the depth tier you dug to: gold pays far more than stone. Push toward the deep gold seams.
  • Reveal 💧 water and it floods the next turn, blocking mined-out neighbours. Reveal 💨 gas and it locks the 4 neighbours for one turn, then disperses. A cell whose stack runs out is mined out (a gap — not chainable).

Why it is ours: The mine already has hazards and tools; this adds the one dimension a flat grid lacks — DEPTH. Clearing becomes excavating, with risk/reward for digging deeper. It reuses ore families and the hazard vocabulary, but the board now has a hidden third axis.
