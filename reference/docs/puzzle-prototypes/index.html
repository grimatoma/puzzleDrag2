Puzzle Prototypes

Ten playable expansions of our one core verb — drag a chain of touching same-type tiles — exploring what new concepts we can add that are unique to our game. Ordered near → far: from safe, shippable twists to bold reframings of what the board even is. Inspired in part by how Puzzle Craft 2 grew a near-identical drag-match game by bolting on a water board.

10 playable demos one shared engine drag & play in your browser ★ #4 = the PC2 water-board homage

Each tile below is a real, draggable prototype — not a mockup. They share one design system and one tiny drag/grid engine (proto.css + proto.js), so every idea looks and feels like the same game while isolating one new mechanical seam. Tap ▶ Play on any card.

The verb we're expanding

The core loop today

Drag across 3+ touching tiles of the same family (8 directions) → release to harvest → tiles clear, the board cascades + refills, and you accrue that family's resource and nudge a tile-tier upgrade. That's the whole verb. It's clean, it's tactile, and it's the thing we don't want to break.

A deliberate, hard-won lesson lives here: a second, different puzzle verb (the removed swap-match "Fiber Crush") fragmented attention. So every prototype here keeps the one drag gesture and changes what it means — never a second minigame.

Depth the game already has

  • Cross-collect — partner families adjacent to a chain auto-harvest (grass↔grain, fruit↔tree…).
  • Hazards — rats & fire (farm); cave-ins, gas, lava (mine) lock or spread across cells.
  • Mine tools — area blast, transform-adjacent, transform-all reshape the board outside the drag.
  • Bosses & seasons — raise the minimum chain length, change the tile pool over a run.

These prototypes lean on this vocabulary — adjacency, cascade, refill, hazards, terrain — rather than inventing whole new systems.

What Puzzle Craft 2's water board teaches us

PC2 is the closest commercial cousin to our game — a drag-to-match harvesting puzzle feeding a settlement economy. Its big mid-game expansion wasn't a new genre; it was a third board: the sea. Studying how it differs from its own land board is the richest source of "what could ours add". Five levers stand out:

⛵
A mover on the board. The land board has no avatar — you just match. The sea adds a boat that occupies a cell and must be steered. Suddenly position matters. → our Tide Lanes & Mycelium Spread.
🎯
Player-directed spawning. Matching deep water makes a fish/island appear at the last tile of your chain — you decide where the next reward lands, then sail to it. Routing becomes planning. → Tide Lanes, Loop Harvest.
🔁
Two-phase turns. A "navigation move" (match water to set up) is distinct from a "ship move" (go collect). Set-up and pay-off split apart. → Tide Lanes, Brew Path, Mycelium.
🗺️
Terrain variety. Shallow water yields salt; deep water yields water; islands block. The board is no longer uniform. → River Run, Deep Layers, Tide Lanes.
🪝
Harvest-by-reaching. You don't collect a fish by matching it — you must move the boat onto it. Collection becomes a spatial act, not a pattern match. → Tide Lanes, Mycelium, Tilt.
Sources: AppGamer — To the Sea guide Gamezebo — PC2 tips PC2 Wiki JayIsGames review

The insight: PC2 didn't add a new gesture — it added new meaning around the same match (a mover, a place to aim, terrain, a two-step rhythm). That's exactly the design budget we want: keep our beloved drag, change what it does. The ten below each pull one of these levers (or invent a sibling).

The ten prototypes

NearSame gesture, same board — a single new dimension. Shippable twists.
01
the time seam

Ripen & Reap

Every tile is alive on a clock: seedling → growing → ripe → rotten. Harvest at peak for the big yield; leave it and it rots and spreads to neighbours. When you chain now matters as much as what.

Why ours: one new per-tile dimension turns a flat match into timing-and-triage — no second verb, reuses cascade/refill exactly.
▶ Play
02
the connection-rule seam

Two-Tone Braid

The connection grammar flips from "same" to "alternating". Weave a chain that strictly alternates two families — A,B,A,B — to spin a premium Blend. Reading interleaved patterns becomes the skill.

Why ours: changes only the rule for what connects; same drag, board and refill, totally new board-reading.
▶ Play
03
the emergence seam

Cross-Pollinate

Route a normal chain to hug a cluster of another family and those bordering tiles graft into premium hybrid blooms ✦ worth triple. New tile types emerge from how you draw.

Why ours: evolves the existing cross-collect adjacency into a discovery/combo layer that grows the tile roster mid-run.
▶ Play
MidA new primitive on the board — a mover, terrain, a shape, a sequence. Where PC2's levers land.
★ PC2 homage
04
mover + spawn-placement

Tide Lanes

PC2's water board, ported to our verb. Drag a chain of water to carve a lane; your boat ⛵ sails it, hauling every fish & clam it passes, and a fresh catch spawns where your lane ends — you decide where the next fish appears.

Why ours: the single biggest lever PC2 pulled — a steerable mover + player-placed spawns + harvest-by-reaching — on our chain.
▶ Play
05
the terrain seam

River Run

A river snakes across the field as terrain. Chains drawn alongside flowing water earn a big flow multiplier — and you can dig new channels to irrigate more of the board. Half match-3, half irrigation planning.

Why ours: adds terrain + a board-editing verb; you shape the board instead of only reacting to it.
▶ Play
06
the shape seam

Loop Harvest

Stop drawing lines — draw a fence. Close a same-family chain into a loop and everything enclosed is reaped at once. Bigger, more diverse enclosures pay off quadratically. The chain becomes a boundary.

Why ours: reinterprets the gesture from a line you collect to an area you enclose — a territory puzzle, zero new input.
▶ Play
07
the sequence seam

Brew Path

The board is the kitchen. Drag a path that picks up ingredients in order; the sequence you trace is matched to a recipe book. 🌾→💧→🌾 = Bread. Cook the exact order for a perfect-brew bonus.

Why ours: promotes our crafting recipes onto the board — "match identical" becomes "compose a recipe".
▶ Play
FarReframes what the board is — a third axis, an idle organism, a physics object. Bold bets.
08
the z-axis seam

Deep Layers

The mine gains a third dimension. Each cell is a stack — clear the top ore and dig down to the richer, more dangerous layer beneath. Stone over iron over gem over gold… but water floods and gas waits in the deep.

Why ours: adds the one dimension a flat grid lacks — depth — reusing the mine's ore families and hazard vocabulary.
▶ Play
09
the territory / idle seam

Mycelium Spread

Plant a spore and grow a living network. Your mycelium auto-harvests everything it touches each tick — passive income — and you drag to direct its growth across the richest ground. Spread vs. starve.

Why ours: the active drag becomes a seed for an idle, spreading layer — chains plant growth that pays out on its own.
▶ Play
10
the board-physics seam

Tilt

What if the drag moved the whole board? Swipe to tilt the field; every tile slides under gravity until it hits a wall, lines of 3+ auto-clear, and everything falls again. 2048 meets match-3, on one gesture.

Why ours: the boldest end — the gesture controls board physics instead of selecting tiles — yet still resolves to our 3+ matching.

How they're built

All ten share one foundation so they read as the same game and stay easy to maintain:

docs/puzzle-prototypes/
├─ proto.css  — warm-paper design system, board/tile/panel vocabulary, family colour classes
├─ proto.js   — tiny PD engine: seeded RNG · grid render · the cross-device drag-path tracker · path overlay · toast/log
└─ <slug>/index.html  × 10  — each owns only its unique rules

Because the fiddly bits (pointer capture, backtracking, the grid) live in proto.js, each prototype is small and focused on its idea. Everything is deterministic (seeded), responsive, and reduced-motion safe. None of this is wired into the live game yet — these are evaluation prototypes for deciding which seam to pursue.
