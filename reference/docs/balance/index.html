What this is

One ledger, read from the player inward

puzzleDrag2 has most of its systems built but no defined flow linking them. This document fixes that on paper: it states the ideal player journey and its pacing first, then works backward into the game systems that must deliver it and the economy that paces it. Balancing follows from the journey, not the other way around.

Goals — captured 2026-06-22

Four objectives, one target player

The headline objective is fixing the progression stall so the journey can actually execute; the other three follow once the spine runs.

1 · Fix the progression stall

The highest-leverage change. Home cannot climb past Outpost today (see the softlock). Unblock the ladder so the campaign can reach mid-game.

2 · Compress the value spread

Realized value-per-tile spans ~10×. Pull marquee resources toward a 3–4× ceiling over the median so no single tile family dominates.

3 · Tune pacing / time-to-content

Land the milestone bands: a steady ~1–2 weeks (≈ 20–35 mid-core sessions) to mid-game, with coins a softer secondary gate.

4 · Reduce grind / friction

Smooth the early loop so the first rungs feel earned — a "balanced middle": cozy surface, light strategy, few hard walls.

The shape of play

  • Target player — mid-core (multiple sessions/day) is the baseline; casual and power players sit on either side.
  • Session shape — roughly 2–3 sessions/day; a session is about one run (~10 turns).
  • Time to mid-game — steady ~1–2 weeks to found a 2nd settlement and grow home to City → about 20–35 mid-core sessions.
  • Feel — a balanced middle: enough tension that choices matter, but few hard walls.

Constraints the design must respect

  • No real-money mechanics (assumed); premium, if ever, only skips real-time timers — never buys puzzle advantage.
  • Save-compatibility — value tweaks are safe; structural/persisted-shape changes need a SAVE_SCHEMA bump + migration.
  • Don't silently break the family-value, campaign, or progression-shape snapshot tests — update them intentionally.

The player's game · gameplay + pacing

The ideal journey, end to end

The committed shape: Town 1 (farm) → expedition → Town 2 (mine + "the combination") → branch into open exploration → the Long Return. A fixed two-zone tutorial that teaches the farm then the mine-with-farm, then an open frontier, then an endgame chase. The principle behind when each system enters: introduce a system the phase after the player feels the want for it, and never before its inputs are producible.

How it's built · working backward from the journey

The systems that realize the journey

Each journey phase is delivered by concrete mechanics. This is the technical spine — what must exist, and how the pieces combine into the central choices of the game.

The core loop — chain → refine → grow

A turn is one chain: drag through 3+ adjacent matching tiles (8-direction, no reuse). At fixed thresholds along the chain an upgraded tile spawns and a resource is credited; raw goods refine at buildings, and the refined economy is spent to grow the town.

Building loadout — how you shape your board

Beyond a town's staple tiles, the buildings you place decide what the board is. Two mechanics combine into the central choice:

  • Building-gated category spawners. A category's producer puts that whole tile category into the spawn pool — a Coop makes birds appear, a Vegetable Patch makes vegetables. The per-session picker then chooses which unlocked categories to run.
  • A demolishable loadout. Plots are scarce, so you choose the rest of the buildings — and can demolish to free a plot and remove its power. The loadout is how you tune your own board.

How a zone grows — the per-zone tier ladder

Each town climbs its own authored ladder. A tier-up is one Hearth upgrade costing escalating local resources; it does exactly two things — adds building plots and unlocks the next batch of building types. There is deliberately no turn-budget or yield change, so the puzzle math stays untouched. Zones gate each other via requiresZoneTier (the quarry needs home at City; the branch zones need the quarry). Player level gates nothing — the zone-tier ladder is the single spine.

The combination — food buys mine turns core to Phase C

Town 2's signature mechanic fuses the two economies: a mine round's turns are bought with food. Spend raw farm goods directly for some turns, or convert them at the Kitchen into supplies for more turns per good. Because raw input always works, the mine can never softlock on "I didn't craft supplies" — crafting is the optimization, not the key. This soft-efficiency pattern is the structural fix for the whole class of softlock bug (see Findings): hard possession gates can deadlock; soft efficiency gradients cannot.

Crafting substance — the one open design fork open

Crafting today is a silent instant button. Giving it weight is agreed; the lever is not. The goal is "a real process," not a monetization timer.

Hazards & bosses

Hazards add urgency the farm lacks — mine cave-ins / gas / moles (Phase C), the rats hazard with Ratcatcher buildings (Phase D, Town 3). Bosses punctuate: the capstone boss closes Town 2 (Phase C); the pit boss tier feeds the endgame (Phase E).

The map

Faucets → converters → sinks

The economy reduces to three columns. Faucets mint coins and resources, converters trade one resource up in value, and sinks drain supply to pace progression. Crucially, coins and resources are two parallel currencies, gated on different axes — healthy coin pacing does not imply healthy progression pacing.

The levers underneath

Core formulas

The real formulas from profile.json, each with a one-line gloss. Every balance argument bottoms out in one of these.

Chain coinssrc/state.ts:454 · ~linear in chain length
max(1, floor(chainLength × tileValue)) + coinHookBonus × coin_gain_mult

Coins per chain scale roughly linearly with how many tiles you chained, weighted by the tile's value; building hooks add a flat bonus and boons apply a multiplier.

Resource accrualsrc/state.ts:392–416 · integer divisor
progress[res] += chainLength ; wholeUnits = floor(progress / TILES_PER_RESOURCE[tile])

Chained tiles add to a per-resource counter; every TILES_PER_RESOURCE tiles yields one inventory unit, remainder carried.

Realized value-per-tilethe dominant-strategy yardstick
valuePerTile = ITEMS[res].value / TILES_PER_RESOURCE[tile]

The coins a tile is actually worth per chained tile. The metric the family-value-spread audit ranks every resource by — the closest thing to a "best strategy" signal.

Founding costsrc/features/zones/data.ts:404 · exponential sink
round(300 × 1.7(k−1)) → 300, 510, 867, 1474, …

The k-th settlement founding costs exponentially more (base 300, growth 1.7). The main long-horizon coin sink.

Net coins / runper-session cash flow
net = chainCoins(this run) − 50 + 25 (+ building bonuses)

A home run nets its chain coins minus the 50 entry plus the 25 season-end bonus → break-even ≈ 25 coins.

Turn budgetsrc/features/zones/data.ts:192
max(1, floor((baseTurns + additive + bonus) × (useFertilizer ? 2 : 1)))

How many actions a run gives. Home baseTurns = 10; fertilizer doubles it. Caps coins-per-run and resources-per-run together.

Headline finding · severity: blocker

The progression softlock

⚠ Home cannot progress past Outpost (tier 0) — a circular hard lock.

Home's first rung (Hamlet) needs crafted bread; bread needs the Bakery, which both costs block (a mine good) and only unlocks at Hamlet — the rung it gates. Mine goods come only from the quarry, and the quarry carries requiresZoneTier {home, tier 3 (City)} — which itself needs mine goods. The dependency eats its own tail. A fresh save has no escape: orders/daily-rewards grant only coins, inventory is siloed per zone, and the market's buy UI is itself mine-gated.

It is now code-derived, not just traced: the progression oracle (src/playtest/progression.ts) computes fresh-save reachability and pins the wall at home Outpost→Hamlet on bread. The live dependency ring, reachable-zone map, and per-zone walls are drawn from the code in the progression dashboard → (kept there so they can't drift; this doc owns the narrative and the fix).

The fix — make Town 1 self-contained on farm goods decided

Re-cost the home tier ladder + the Bakery off all mine goods and unlock the Bakery at Outpost, so the farm→bread→tier-up loop closes on farm goods alone (mine goods first demanded in Town 2). Lever: cartography/data.ts tiers[].upgradeCost + BUILDINGS[].cost. This is the smallest change that flips the oracle RED→green; pair with a modest quarry-gate lowering so the mine opens by mid-Town-1.

The mechanism we build next — close the cross-zone gap

The campaign pacing sim is farm-board-only: it does not model crafting, buildings, or the mine, so it cannot yet validate the journey end-to-end by playing.
