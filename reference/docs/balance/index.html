What this doc is

The balance ledger for a farming economy

puzzleDrag2 is a single-player, turn-based, transitive-economy game: a tile-drag match/merge farm loop with a zone-tier progression spine. You chain tiles for coins and resources, convert them through recipes and board upgrades, and spend them down through zone entry, settlement founding, building, and tier-up sinks. This page is the human-readable mirror of profile.json — the source of truth for the knobs, formulas, economy map, findings, and open questions. When the two disagree, the JSON wins and this page is brought back in sync.

How a balance pass runs

The /game-balance skill (1) interviews the design goals, (2) models the economy as faucets & sinks with explicit formulas, (3) tests against the deterministic harness (npm run playtest), (4) proposes targeted changes tied to the goals and applies them on confirmation, then (5) records the result in the decision log and re-syncs the profile.

Goals — captured 2026-06-22

Four objectives, one target player

The goals interview is done; the answers below now drive every proposed change. The headline objective is fixing the progression stall — the other three follow once the lock is broken.

1 · Fix the progression stall

The single highest-leverage change. Home cannot climb past Outpost today (see the softlock). Unblock the ladder so the campaign can reach mid-game.

2 · Compress the value spread

Realized value-per-tile spans ~10×. Pull marquee resources toward a 3–4× ceiling over the median so no single tile family is the obvious dominant strategy.

3 · Tune pacing / time-to-content

Land the milestone bands: a steady ~1–2 weeks (≈ 20–35 mid-core sessions) to mid-game, with coins a softer secondary gate rather than the wall.

4 · Reduce grind / friction

Smooth the early loop so the first rungs feel earned, not punishing — "balanced middle": cozy surface, light strategy, few hard walls.

The shape of play

  • Target player — mid-core (multiple sessions/day) is the baseline segment; casual and power players sit on either side of it.
  • Session shape — roughly 2–3 sessions/day; a session is about one farm run (~10 turns).
  • Time to mid-game — steady ~1–2 weeks to found a 2nd settlement and grow home to City (tier 3) → about 20–35 mid-core sessions.
  • Feel — a balanced middle: enough economic tension that choices matter, but few hard walls.

Constraints the pass must respect

  • No real-money mechanics (assumed; to confirm in the interview).
  • Save-compatibility — value tweaks are safe, but structural/persisted-shape changes need a SAVE_SCHEMA bump + migration.
  • Don't silently break the family-value or campaign snapshot tests — update them intentionally when a change is meant to move them.

Headline finding · severity: blocker

The progression softlock

⚠ Home cannot progress past Outpost (tier 0) — a circular hard lock.

Home's tier ladder needs mine goods (block / iron_bar) from the first real rung. Mine goods come only from the quarry. But the quarry carries requiresZoneTier {zone: home, tier: 3 (City)} — and reaching home→City itself needs mine goods. The dependency eats its own tail. A fresh save has no mine goods in its starting inventory, and the market cannot buy them, so the player is stuck at home Outpost with no path forward.

Read it as a loop: climb home → need block → need the quarry → need home at City → climb home. There is no entry point. Even completing home to found the next settlement needs ~4 buildings that each demand block.

Confirmed: it is a hard lock, not just awkward ordering

CARTO/TRAVEL enforces the gate. At src/features/cartography/slice.ts:78 the reducer returns state unchanged when zoneTierGateReason fails — so a fresh save cannot even travel to the quarry, let alone mine it, until home is City. The player is hard-stuck at home Outpost.

Residual uncertainty

Story/quest grants have not yet been traced — it is conceivable a quest seeds early block. A fresh-save manual playtest would confirm the lock 100%. Everything else points to a hard wall.

Likely cause & the lever

This is almost certainly a side-effect of PR #1299 (zones-as-spine) not reconciling the authored home tier/building costs against the new gate. It reads as a regression, not an intended wall.

The lever — src/features/cartography/data.ts:344

The quarry's requiresZoneTier {zone: home, tier: 3}. Lower / re-gate it so the mine opens early, and/or re-cost the early home tiers + tier-0 buildings so the first rungs don't demand mine goods before the mine is reachable. Either edit breaks the cycle.

The map

Faucets → converters → sinks

The whole economy reduces to three columns. Faucets mint coins and resources, converters trade one resource up in value, and sinks drain supply to pace progression. Balance lives in the ratios between them — and crucially, coins and resources are two parallel currencies, gated on different axes.

Faucets

  • Chain collection coins — the primary income, per chain resolved on the board.
  • Resource accrual — chained tiles accrue whole inventory units.
  • Season-end bonus — flat +25 coins at CLOSE_SEASON.
  • Daily rewards — login-streak grants.
  • Order / market sales — coin income for resources.

Converters

  • Recipes — craft resources into goods/tools, station-gated.
  • Board upgradeMap — chaining a tile redirects spawns to a higher-tier tile.

Sinks

  • Zone entry — 50/session at home.
  • Tier-up — resource-gated (the softlock surface).
  • Settlement founding — coin-gated, exponential.
  • Building cost + tool charges.

The levers underneath

Core formulas

The real formulas from profile.json, each with a one-line gloss. Every balance argument ultimately bottoms out in one of these.

Chain coinssrc/state.ts:454 · ~linear in chain length
max(1, floor(chainLength × tileValue)) + coinHookBonus × coin_gain_mult

Coins per chain scale roughly linearly with how many tiles you chained, weighted by the tile's value; building hooks add a flat bonus and boons apply a multiplier. Floor, minimum 1.

Resource accrualsrc/state.ts:392–416 · integer divisor
progress[res] += chainLength ; wholeUnits = floor(progress / TILES_PER_RESOURCE[tile])

Chained tiles add to a per-resource progress counter; every TILES_PER_RESOURCE tiles yields one whole inventory unit, with the remainder carried to the next chain.

Realized value-per-tilethe dominant-strategy yardstick
valuePerTile = ITEMS[res].value / TILES_PER_RESOURCE[tile]

The coins a tile is actually worth once you account for how many tiles it takes to make a unit. This is the distance-from-power-curve metric the family-value-spread audit ranks every resource by — the closest thing to a "best strategy" signal.

Founding costsrc/features/zones/data.ts:404 · exponential sink
round(300 × 1.7(k−1)) → 300, 510, 867, 1474, …

The k-th settlement founding costs exponentially more than the last (base 300, growth 1.7). The main long-horizon coin sink, designed to keep coins meaningful late.

Net coins / runper-session cash flow
net = chainCoins(this run) − 50 + 25 (+ building bonuses)

A home run nets its chain coins minus the 50 entry cost plus the 25 season-end bonus, so the break-even chain income for a home session is about 25 coins.

Turn budgetsrc/features/zones/data.ts:192
max(1, floor((baseTurns + additive + bonus) × (useFertilizer ? 2 : 1)))

How many actions a run gives you. Home baseTurns = 10; fertilizer doubles the budget. Every chain spends a turn, so this caps coins-per-run and resources-per-run together.

Two axes, balanced separately. Coins (chain income → entry / founding / building sinks) and resources (accrual → recipes / tier-up sinks) are parallel currencies. Healthy coin pacing does not imply healthy progression pacing — see the softlock.

Objective 1 in depth — three ways to break the lock

The lock has two interlocking gates, both routing through block: the travel gate (requiresZoneTier, blocks reaching the quarry) and the founding / completion gate (founding the next settlement needs home "completed" = ~4 buildings, most needing block). Any fix must clear a farm-only path to the mine.

Option 1 — Open the mine earlier

Lower the quarry's gate at cartography/data.ts:344 from {home, tier 3} → {home, tier 1} (or tier 0).

PRO one-line lever; the mine becomes reachable before home needs mine goods.

CON not sufficient alone — reaching Hamlet and completing home still need block via Bakery→bread. Also shifts the "mine = late unlock" pacing.

Option 2 — Farm-affordable early path REC. CORE

Drop mine goods from the first buildings so home grows + completes from farm goods; keep mine goods on the upper rungs. In constants.ts:

  • Bakery {plank:8, block:6, eggs:4} → {plank:8, hay_bundle:6, eggs:4} — the keystone: bread becomes farm-craftable.
  • Mill {plank:6, bread:8, block:2, iron_bar:8} → {plank:6, bread:8}.
  • Granary {bread:12, block:6, iron_bar:4} → {bread:12, plank:6}.

PRO preserves zone-unlock order; cozy farm-driven opening (fits "balanced middle"); Village/City still need mine goods.

CON touches a few costs; needs the quarry open before Village → pair with a modest Option 1.

Option 3 — Early mine-good faucet

Seed a small starter stock of block/iron_bar, let the market buy a capped amount, or grant some via an early quest/daily.

PRO least structural change to gates/costs.

CON a band-aid; "block from nowhere" undercuts the mine's purpose and can distort the value spread you also want to fix.

Recommendation — Option 2 + a modest Option 1

Re-cost Bakery + Mill + Granary to farm-only and open the quarry at {home, tier 1} (Hamlet) instead of City. That yields a clean, intent-preserving loop:

Outpost → (farm → bakery → bread) → Hamlet → quarry opens → mine block/coke/silver → Village → City → found #2

It breaks the circle, keeps the mine a mid-progression unlock, and leaves City / premium tiers needing mine goods. Validate with a fresh-save playthrough after applying.

First tooling task — close the harness gap.

The campaign sim is farm-board-only: it does not model crafting, buildings, or the mine, so it cannot yet validate the lock fix end-to-end. Extend it (or run a manual trace) to model the building/crafting/mine path before claiming objective 1 is fixed. Without it, the milestone bands stay theoretical.
