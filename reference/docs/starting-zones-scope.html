Starting Zones 1 & 2 — Scope & Balance Baseline

The complete, deliberately-narrowed set of buildings, recipes, tools, tiles, costs and thresholds that exist in the first two settlements — and an explicit list of everything to ignore while balancing them.

Town 1 · Farm Town 2 · Farm + Mining values = PROPOSED source of truth = src/

00 Purpose & how to read

The game currently ships far more concepts than the early game should expose — 32 buildings, 50 recipes, 80+ tiles, 30+ worker types, 10 tile families. This document fixes the scope of zones 1 and 2 so balancing has a finite surface: a fixed list of what a new player can build, craft, hold, and chain, with one proposed number on every knob.

IN SCOPE shipped & balanced now CHANGED differs from current code DEFERRED exists, but made unreachable here
Two ground rules

1 — Every town has a farm. Mining/fishing are additive layers on top. 2 — Nothing out of scope gets a flag. We keep deferred content in the catalog (art still renders) but simply don't make its building buildable or its recipe/tile unlockable. The Out of Scope tab is the ignore-list for balancing.

02 The caravan — founding Town 2

Founding currently costs 300 × 1.7^k coins (settlementFoundingCost, features/zones/data.ts). We replace that with a one-time resource caravan paid out of farm production — consistent with the rule that big commitments are a demand on your goods, not your purse. In scope there is exactly one founding:

06 Buildings — powers & costs

Thirteen building types across both towns. Costs re-pointed onto in-scope resources and sequenced so early (pre-mine) buildings cost farm goods only; metal-costed buildings naturally gate behind having a mine. CHANGED marks a power or cost altered from current code.

Unlocks are global

Once any town's tier ladder unlocks a building type, you can build it at every town you own (plots permitting) — it is never re-locked per zone. The Unlocked by + Tier columns show only where each type first becomes available; a "Both · 3 / 2" entry unlocks at whichever of the two ladders you reach first.

Tier = the rung of that town's ladder where the building unlocks (0 = Outpost/Camp · 1 = Hamlet/Diggings · 2 = Village/Boomtown · 3 = Town). "3 / 2" = tier 3 in Town 1, tier 2 in Town 2. The ladders are in the Towns & Buildings tab.

House replaces housing / housing2 / housing3

Today three identical Housing Blocks chain off each other. We collapse them into one repeatable House, capped per town (3 at Town 1 → 9 pop; 2 at Town 2 → 6 pop). Each grants a fixed +3 villager capacity on build (proposed, replacing the +1/season drip). Villagers are spent 1:1 to hire workers.

Why these costs changed (drop-ins from out-of-scope resources)

Current code prices several of these in resources we've cut from zones 1–2: Chapel costs gold_bar + ink; Sawmill leaned on coke; Granary/Mining Camp on dirt/iron before a mine exists. Each is re-pointed onto an in-scope good of similar worth. Apiary (boosts pansy→honey) and Stable (boosts horse→horseshoe) are dropped entirely — their target tiles (flowers, mounts) are out of scope.

07 How the board pays — chain & yield rules

Cross-collect

Drag 3+ of one family → collect → the family's raw resource drops into stores and pays coins (≈ tiles × value). Chain to the family's bloom threshold N and a next-tier tile blooms where you let go. Adjacent partner families get cross-collected.

Excluded from the first zones

Flowers (Pansy → honey) and Mounts (Horse → horseshoe) stay out per rule — flipped off default. Cattle (Cow → milk) also deferred. Herd is back in (Pig, above). Every other chain/research/buy/daily unlock hanging off the starters (Wheat, Pear, Birch, Hen, Eggplant…) is re-gated to a later zone so only the 2 discoveries below are reachable.

The Birds→Herd bloom — resolved

The home board blooms birds into herd animals (upgradeMap in cartography/data.ts). With herd back in scope the bloom stays, and Pig is the 7th starter that receives it — no redirect needed.

12 Mine expeditions — rations

A mine session at Town 2 is paid in food carried from the farm. Any edible item is a ration — there is no special "Supplies" item. The more processed the food, the more turns it buys, so crafted goods are more turn-efficient than raw produce. You must pack at least 12 turns of food to set out (MIN_EXPEDITION_TURNS = 12 CHANGED).

Any food → turns, by processing tier (PROPOSED)

Rules
  • Min to set out: 12 turns of packed food
  • Any food item works — no "Supplies"
  • More processed → more turns (raw 1 · staple 2 · rich 3)
  • Mining Camp: +1 turn to every mine food
  • Larder: +1 mine-food turn (stacks)
  • Mine entry toll: 100 coins on top of food

e.g. 12 apples, or 6 bread, or 4 cured meats — or any mix totalling 12 turns. Building bonuses stack on top (Mining Camp + Larder makes a cured meat worth 5). Richer deferred rations — festival loaf, wedding pie, iron ration — arrive with zone 3+.

15 Out of scope — the ignore list

Everything below exists in the codebase but is deliberately unreachable in zones 1–2. When balancing, treat these as out of the model — don't tune them, don't let them into the economy.
