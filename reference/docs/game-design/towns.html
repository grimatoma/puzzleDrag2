Founding by caravan

You open a new town by outfitting a caravan and rolling it out to a site on the map. The caravan is paid in coins — the global cash layer, never a town's own materials, so founding can never depend on a place you have not opened yet. (The Caravan Yard you raise in Town 1 makes every later founding cheaper.)

Founding is non-linear, gated only by reach and coins. You can open any site the map lets you reach — one adjacent to a town you have already settled — as soon as you can afford it; you do not have to finish the town you are in first. Several towns can be in progress at once, and you switch between them freely; if a town is not grabbing you, leave it and develop another, then come back. The coin cost rises with each town you open, and the map's reach expands outward from where you have settled — so the harder, farther sites naturally come later. The order is a cozy unfurling, never a forced sequence. Home is where you start, auto-founded for free; every town after is caravan-founded.

The seven-town map

Seven towns, all required for the finale but openable in any order the map allows. Every town adds at most one new board (Farm → Mine → Fish), one crux, one win task (varied — not always a boss), and a thematic signature building. The left-to-right ordering below is the frontier the map suggests as difficulty ramps — not a forced sequence.

Sizes are the design target. Plot counts and costs are tuned in the Balance Profile and the playtest harness.

Unlock order is strategy. Each town's signature building, once that town is complete, can be raised in your other towns for a cross-zone bonus — a Pit Canteen adds +2 ration-turns in any mine; a Supply Depot adds an idle drip anywhere. So choosing which town to finish first is a real decision, not just a sequence. The full export roster is in Buildings & tools.

Town 1 Hearthwood Vale — the farm

Boards
Farm only — the free base board.
Crux
The tutorial hub. Teaches every verb — chain, refine, fill orders, raise a House and hire a worker, tier up — with no pressure. Your home forever.
Hook
None by design: the gentle teacher.
Size
~6 slots — so you already feel the build choice: Mill / Bakery / Kitchen / Granary fill most of it; one or two left for a House or a yield building.
Win task
Build your first caravan (at the Caravan Yard) — this opens the map, so you can roll out and found any reachable site. The win is the bridge onward.
  1. First chains. Drag 3+ touching tiles; each banks crops and, if long, merges a tile up a tier.
  2. Refine. Grain → Mill → flour → Bakery → bread; Field Kitchen turns grain into rations; Granary stores it.
  3. People & workers. Raise a House for Villagers, then hire your first worker (see hiring).
  4. Build the caravan. Save up the coins, outfit your caravan at the Caravan Yard, and roll out to your first new site (Two Rivers sits just over the ridge).

Town 2 Two Rivers — the connection

Boards
Farm Mine — the first stacked town, one shared stockpile.
Crux
The whole point: the farm feeds the mine. A calm valley where farm food first buys mine expeditions; the mine pays out ore; ore refines into metal. No hazards — just learn the link.
Hook
None — no hazards. The safe town where the supply chain clicks.
Size
~10 slots.
Win task
Fill a grand order — a shipment needing both bread and iron, proving you can run both boards. Its signature Quartermaster's Hall exports better food→ration packing to later mines.
  1. Found & bootstrap. Caravan in; workers, tools and Villagers came with you, materials start empty.
  2. Run the farm board. Free turns: chain grain, build the Field Kitchen, make rations.
  3. Open the mine. Spend rations to depart; chain stone and iron; keep everything mined when food runs out.
  4. Refine & connect. Smelter ore → metal; watch the farm refill the pantry while the mine empties it.
  5. Fill the grand order. Assemble bread + iron, complete it, build the caravan, roll on.

Towns 3–7 — the full required path

Town 3 Cracked Quarry — hazards arrive

Boards
Farm Mine — same stack, now dangerous.
Crux
The farm→mine loop under pressure: cave-ins, gas, moles eat tiles and force efficient chains plus hazard tools (the Powderworks' bombs).
Win task
Defeat the capstone boss (e.g. Old Stoneface). Signature Pit Canteen (+2 ration-turns underground) exports to any mine — a prime build-order pick.

Town 4 Saltspray Harbor — the fishery

Boards
Farm Mine Fish — the full stack: fishing needs farm bait + mine tackle.
Crux
The supply chain at full depth; tides shift the board.
Win task
The Regatta — a festival (cured-fish haul + a built Wharf), not a fight. Signature Bait Shack exports +fishing.

Town 5 Greenmeadow — the rich farm & the rats

Boards
Farm Mine — a deep farm with its own mine.
Crux
Abundance is the threat: as the stockpile climbs, rats eat adjacent crops — farm-side hazard management while you still run the mine.
Win task
Raise a great building (Cathedral / Great Granary) on a deep basket while keeping rats down. Signature Cattery exports rat protection (a Cat tool) to any farm.

Town 6 Deepdelve — the mine with no farm

Boards
Mine only — breaks the stacking rule on purpose.
Crux
The planning town. No farm, so you ration a scarce, capped supply income: Supply Depots drip rations every few hours, and you ship baskets in by caravan from your farm towns. Sequencing, not grinding.
Win task
Fill the great shipment under that scarcity. Signature Supply Depot exports the idle drip everywhere — the most coveted cross-zone unlock.

Town 7 The Old Capital — the finale

Boards
Farm Mine Fish — everything, largest scale.
Crux
The capstone. Requires all six prior towns complete; brings the whole toolkit plus every exported building to bear.
Win task
Build the grand Monument — the longest basket in the game. Finishing it wins the run.
Varied win tasks. Caravan-build, grand order, boss, regatta, great building, great shipment, monument — completion never feels rote, and the finale is gated on all six prior towns.
