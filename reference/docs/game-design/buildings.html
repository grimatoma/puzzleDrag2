Common, specialized & clear-named

Two kinds of buildings, and every name says what it does:

  • Common — the board's core chain (Mill, Bakery, Smelter, Wharf…), available in any town with that board. These are the must-builds.
  • Specialized — one thematic signature per town, tied to its core idea. Once that town is complete, its signature can be raised in your other towns for a cross-zone bonus — so unlock order is strategy (see The seven towns).

Slots < buildings — the build choice

After the core chain, only a few discretionary slots remain — and Houses compete for them too (more Villagers vs. more abilities). The gap widens as towns grow: slots lag the building menu, so later towns force harder, sharper specialization. Because a building unlocks its tool globally and permanently (see Tools), the choice is about pacing and per-town identity, not permanent loss.

Houses & removal

Houses → Villagers

Each House produces Villagers, the global worker-currency pool. How hiring spends them lives in Systems → hiring; here, a House simply costs a slot.

The slot tension

A House competes with ability and yield buildings: a wide worker base, or rich abilities? A real per-town call.

Removal rule

Tear down discretionary buildings to re-plan a town. Two limits: major / functionality-unlocking buildings are permanent once built (the Hearth, board-openers, a signature, anything that unlocked new functionality); and a House cannot be removed if it would drop Villagers below the number in use — build one elsewhere first, since the pool is global. Building upgrades (levelling a building for a stronger effect) are a parked, later concept.
