1 · The fantasy & this document

The fantasy. You are the descendant of a founder who, a century ago, banked the hearth of Hearthwood Vale rather than letting it die — and swore one of their line would return. You are that one. You arrive in spring with nothing but the oath, and you rebuild the kingdom one ruin at a time: clear the rubble, climb a settlement's tier ladder by chaining → refining → building, then raise the wagon that wakes the next ruin. The central verb is remember, not conquer. The whole arc culminates — much later — in renewing the Pact at the Old Capital.

What this document is. The old session repeatedly mistook the code for "missing" things that were present, or trusted stale numbers. So this doc now serves two jobs:

  • The World — a verified, system-by-system account of what is actually coded today (counts, values, file refs, and a wired / partial / stub / doc-only status on each). This is the part that lets a future session understand the game without paging through src/.
  • The Plan — the v2 progression we intend to build, expressed as deltas against that world.

Where the two disagree, code is truth — but this doc is meant to stay in sync with the code, so when a system changes, update its World section in the same change.

The core loop & the supply chain

Town 1 has no mine. Following PC2 (village 1 = farm only; the mine appears at village 2): Town 1 = Farm only · Town 2 = Farm + Mine · Town 3 = Farm + Mine (deeper) · sea later. The mine board, Kitchen/supplies, the food→mine gate, metal buildings, and the capacity ladder are all Town 2 introductions.

3 · Story & setting DOC-ONLY

The fiction from docs/the_long_return_master_doc_v3.md (v3.1, ~1385 lines). Almost none of it is mechanically enforced yet — treat as the narrative target. Beats 1/3/4 (arrival, naming, first harvest) and Mira's Bond-8 letter are the parts actually in src/story.ts.

Premise

A century ago Hearthwood Vale emptied in one bad winter. Its founders banked the hearth and vowed a return. You arrive as that descendant. As your smoke rises, sister settlements quiet for 100 years stir. The kingdom is "the rediscovery of a dormant network of vows" — a portfolio of single-purpose settlements (Farm / Mine / Harbor) sharing one economy. The opening: a letter from Wren, the 70-year-old sole hearthkeeper (52 years tending the ember alone): "The ember still burns. I am tired. Come home, if you mean to." Her first act is to hand you the tongs.

The world & the Hollow Folk's Pact

The Hearthwood is a fertile valley — mountains N/E, marsh S, the Saltspray Sea W — once a federation of ~11 holds bound by a founding Charter and a centennial gathering at the Old Capital. The land was made habitable by the Hollow Folk (slow, lantern-bearing, speak only in tally-marks) under a Pact — 6 terms: (1) the hearths shall not go out; (2) renew the Pact each century; (3) sister-holds share labor; (4) names shall be kept; (5) the keepers shall be honored; (6) the Ember shall not be moved. The abandonment was mundane, not cursed.

The Keepers & the central choice

Keepers are aspects of the land made conscious — the Deer-Spirit (farm), the Stone-Knocker (mine), the Tidesinger (harbor), plus the Bramble-Folk (forest chorus). Each settlement's defining, final decision at its outer hearth:

The Ember finale

At the Old Capital the original Ember "reads your record" — which settlement you founded first, who you Coexisted vs Drove Out, finished NPC arcs, your Ember/Ingot ratio — and gates the Charter renewal (all-Coexist → "Living Charter"; all-Drive-Out → "Sovereign Charter"; mixed → standard). Fill precedence (each empty cell, in order)

  1. Pending chain-upgrade tile for that column (non-initial fills) — placed via nextUpgradeTile reading zone.upgradeMap.
  2. _pickFromZoneSeasonDrops() (farm only; returns null off-farm or when the zone has no season table).
  3. _randomFromPool() fallback — rollResource(pool) = uniform pick over a repetition-weighted array (a key listed 3× is 3× as likely; there is no separate weight field).

Pool modifiers applied inside fillBoard (order): applyPoolWeightAdds (worker/building pool_weight copies) → session category filter (farm; player's Start-Farming checkboxes) → boss spawnBias → fertilizer bias → applySeasonPoolMods (SEASON_POOL_MODS: Spring +blackberry, Summer +wheat, Autumn +oak×2, Winter +stone/−hay). activePool() substitutes each category slot with the player's active tile for that category.

Turn budget & in-session seasons

turnBudgetForZone = max(1, floor((baseTurns + bonuses) × mult)). baseTurns: home/meadow/quarry 10, orchard/caves/forge/harbor 12; fertilizer doubles. A single session is split into 4 equal seasons by turn budget (not 4 sessions): seasonIndexInSession quarters the budget, picking which seasonDrops column is live. The calendar-year season system was removed in Phase 7 — only these spawn-weight tweaks + visuals remain.

The giant-chain question, answered with code + math. Because 1 chain = 1 turn regardless of length and there is no max-chain cap, the turn counter limits how many chains, never how long one is. On a 6×6 king's-graph the expected largest 8-connected same-type group depends on the number of effective tile types N (derived percolation estimate, not a coded value):
Effective types Np = 1/NLargest same-type groupFeel
2.50~12–20near board-spanning sweeps — "one giant drag"
3.33~6–12still long
4.25~4–7healthy
6.17~3–4chains hover at the minimum
10.10~3.6frequent auto-shuffles
The throttle is T, not the turn counter: a 20-hay sweep (T=6) costs 1 turn but pays only 3 bundles + 3 upgrades + 10 coins — a fantasy moment, not an economy break. So the fix for a too-sweepy opening is pool width (open at ~4 effective categories), never a chain cap. The §29 sim invariant "max natural chain on the Stage-0 pool ≤ ~8" guards this.

How selling actually works — and the bread inconsistency. The Market sell payout is round(value × 0.10) (SELL_RATE = 0.10, features/market/pricing.ts) — not the raw value, and not MARKET_PRICES. So:
  • bread.value = 125 → sells for round(125×0.10) = 13 coins. The item description "sold for 125 coins at the Bakery" is misleading.
  • MARKET_PRICES.bread = {buy:60, sell:4} is not the payout — it's only the baseline that driftPrices (market.ts:44) jitters ±15% for the buy/sell display. It's inconsistent with the value-based payout and should be reconciled.
The "Bakery ≈ 5× multiplier" still holds as a ratio (bread value 125 vs 3×flour value 24 ≈ 5×), because the 0.10 factor applies to both — but absolute sell prices in older notes were 10× too high.

26 · Balance math & ratios

Chain of length L, tile threshold T, tile value v:

  • Upgrade tiles = floor(L / T)
  • Resource credited = 1 per T tiles (fractional carry in resourceProgress)
  • Coins = floor(L × v / 2)
  • Market sell of a resource = round(value × 0.10) (§7)

T is the giant-chain throttle: output scales as L/T, so a huge early chain feels great but pays little (§5).

29 · Worked economy & the giant-chain answer

The inflection points

  • 1 flour = 5 wheat tiles (T=5). A 5-wheat chain (v=2) also pays floor(5×2/2)=5 coins.
  • 1 bread = 3 flour + 1 egg = 15 wheat + 6 bird tiles → value 125 (sells 13). Ratio vs raw flour ≈ 5× — the first economic inflection (Town 1).
  • 1 supplies = 5 flour = 25 wheat tiles → 1 expedition turn. 1 mine trip (3 turns) = 75 wheat tiles. The throttle; sim candidate supplies = flour×3 (45 tiles).

Stage-0 width — the decision the giant-chain analysis forces

Because 1 chain = 1 turn and there's no cap (§5), a 2-category opening = board-spanning sweeps (12–20 tiles). That's not an economy break (T throttles reward) but it's a poor feel. Recommendation (taken): open at ~3–4 effective categories — grass + grain + 1–2 low-weight "texture" fillers so the first drag isn't a half-board sweep; spawner buildings raise the rest from 0 via pool_weight. The sim invariant max natural chain on the Stage-0 pool ≤ ~8 guards it.

32 · The deferred layer — net-new reward homes

The principle that unblocks the meta. "What's the reward for keepers/bosses when global rewards are already strong?" is a supply problem — the power economy is saturated, so any new power reward is redundant or game-breaking. Add new demand, not supply: build a net-new system the player wants to feed, and the meta rewards get a home that's impossible to get elsewhere. Don't run a parallel multiplier economy.
  • The "third pillar" slot (PC2's sea) → our own thing, likely a collection / curation pillar. Coexist/Drive-Out becomes which collection you build: a living menagerie of spared spirits vs a trophy hall of bested ones. Prestige & completion — non-breaking.
  • Keepers return as the settlement capstone (after Town 1) → a qualitative board-rule fork + the Hearth-Token.
  • Old Capital = the endgame restoration sink; the Charter "reads your record."
  • Parked seeds: settlements that buff each other once restored; a map that visibly reconnects; a "pick which ruin next" menu.
