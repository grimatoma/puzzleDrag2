What this is

This is the place a designer reviews the game's economy with another designer. It pairs with profile.json (the source of truth for knobs, formulas, the economy map, targets, findings, and open questions) — this page mirrors that file in readable form. When the two disagree, the JSON wins; this page is brought back in sync after every pass.

puzzleDrag2 is a single-player, turn-based farming game built on a transitive economy: you chain tiles on a board to earn coins and accrue resources, convert those through recipes and board upgrades, and spend them down through zone entry, settlement founding, building, and tier-up sinks. The job of balance here is to keep that loop paced and legible — not to dial difficulty against an opponent, but to tune time-to-content and avoid a single dominant strategy.

How to use this doc. Run a balance pass with the /game-balance skill. It (1) interviews the design goals first — teaching balance concepts as it asks — then (2) models the economy as faucets and sinks with explicit formulas, (3) tests with the deterministic harness (npm run playtest), (4) proposes targeted changes tied to the goals, applies them on confirmation, and re-tests, and finally (5) records the decision here and updates profile.json. The decision log is the running history of that work.

Economy map

The whole economy reduces to three columns. Faucets mint coins and resources, converters transform one resource into a more valuable one, and sinks drain the supply to pace progression. Balance lives in the ratios between them.

Faucets

  • Chain collection coins — the primary income, per chain resolved on the board.
  • Resource accrual — chained tiles accrue whole inventory units.
  • Season-end bonus — flat +25 coins at CLOSE_SEASON.
  • Daily rewards — login-streak grants.
  • Market sales — order / market coin income for resources.

Converters & sinks

  • Recipes — craft resources into goods/tools, station-gated.
  • Board upgradeMap — chaining a tile redirects spawns to a higher-tier tile.
  • Sinks — zone entry (50/session at home), tier-up (resource-gated), settlement founding (coin-gated, exponential), building cost, tool charges.

Core formulas

The real formulas from profile.json, each with a one-line plain-English gloss. These are the levers every balance argument ultimately bottoms out in.

chain coins · src/state.ts:454~ linear in chain length
max(1, floor(chainLength × tileValue)) + coinHookBonus × coin_gain_mult

Coins per chain scale roughly linearly with how many tiles you chained, weighted by the tile's value; building hooks add a flat bonus and boons apply a multiplier. Floor, min 1.

resource accrual · src/state.ts:392–416integer divisor
wholeUnits = floor(progress / TILES_PER_RESOURCE[tile])

Chained tiles add to a per-resource progress counter; every TILES_PER_RESOURCE tiles yields one whole inventory unit, with the remainder carried to the next chain.

realized value-per-tile · the dominant-strategy metricaudit yardstick
valuePerTile = ITEMS[res].value / TILES_PER_RESOURCE[tile]

The coins a tile is actually worth once you account for how many tiles it takes to make a unit. This is the distance-from-power-curve yardstick the family-value-spread audit ranks every resource by — the closest thing the game has to a "best strategy" signal.

founding cost · src/features/zones/data.ts:404exponential sink
round(300 × 1.7(k−1)) → 300, 510, 867, 1474, …

The k-th settlement founding costs exponentially more than the last (base 300, growth 1.7). This is the main long-horizon coin sink, designed to keep coins meaningful late.

net coins / run · per-run cash flowper session
net = chainCoins(this run) − 50 + 25 (+ building bonuses)

A home run nets its chain coins minus the 50 entry cost plus the 25 season-end bonus. The break-even chain income for a home session is therefore ~25 coins.

Two axes, balanced separately. Coins (chain income → entry / founding / building sinks) and resources (accrual → recipes / tier-up sinks) are parallel currencies. Healthy coin pacing does not imply healthy progression pacing — see the progression stall finding.

Progression stall headline finding

A farm-only home campaign stalls before tier 1 (Hamlet). Coins are plentiful, but the tier ladder is gated on resources the farm board cannot produce. The only missing input for Hamlet is crafted bread ×10. The next rung, Village (tier 2), further requires cross-zone resources — block, coke, and silver_bar — that the home farm never spawns. Progression is therefore gated across subsystems, on a different axis from the (healthy) coin curve.

Cross-subsystem tier gate investigate

The home tier ladder is gated on crafted (bread) and cross-zone (block / coke / silver_bar) resources from tier 1 onward; a farm-only loop stalls before tier 1. Confirm this cross-subsystem gating is intended.
