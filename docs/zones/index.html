The Zone Atlas — Ten Worlds That Grow

Ten unique, AAA-bar settlement zones for puzzleDrag2 — each a different world, each growing from a foothold into a flourishing place by its own logic of growth. Each zone is a different environment and a different logic of growth — built on the town-layout foundation (roads-first, the wilderness as the progress bar, a landmark that levels up in place), then taken somewhere new. The art here is a flat-color / vector mockup; pixel art is generated once a layout locks.

Heads up — this is the earlier exotic concept set. These ten fantasy zones (volcano, sky-isles, crystal caverns, haunted ruins…) were later reworked into grounded, realistic frontier settlements — believable towns in the woods, plains, hills and coast that read like real growing places. → See the Grounded Settlements hub. Both sets live here in the docs; the grounded set is the current direction.

This atlas proposes ten settlement zones for puzzleDrag2. Every one is unique in look and in how it grows — a stilt-town strung over black water, a forge-town terraced up a live volcano, a city of crystal caverns, a sky-port on floating isles, a haunted ruin you reclaim from the fog. They share one craft bar (below) and the same authored-map contract as the home town, so each can become a real, playable, growing place. Drag the “Grow” slider on any card to watch a foothold become a capital (double-click to let it breathe on its own).

How they grow — ten different layout logics

Variety of layout style is a hard requirement: no two zones grow the same way. Each claims exactly one growth topology — the menu of ten is the spine of the whole set.

Fenmead — brewed at the Fenmead Brewery (unlocked at Mire Village) — an end-good you sell or spend, gating the final rung once you can make it
Signature mechanic

The tide cycle IS the game: at high tide only the upper boardwalk rows are workable; at low tide the harbour opens below. You bank every build and chain around the rising and falling water — reading the tide, not the board, is the skill.

Hazards — reused from the game + new
reuse Tidewater flood — Water rises each in-run season, submerging the lowest board rows (tiles become unchainable). Counter: Chain to drain — clearing tiles on a flooded row pumps it back; bogwalk pylons protect adjacent rows.
reuse Miasma fog — A poison cloud blooms from a vent and spreads; tiles caught in it spoil after 3 turns. Counter: Chain any tiles in the cloud before the timer to disperse it (gas-vent rules).
new Leech bloom — Leeches latch onto a cluster of tiles and multiply, eating 1 plant/fish tile each per turn. Counter: Salt-chain: a 4+ chain of any kind purges leeches in its footprint for coins.
new Will-o'-wisp lure — A wisp marks a high-value tile and drags it one cell toward the deep water each turn; lost if it reaches the edge. Counter: Chain the marked tile to break the lure (bonus coins if you do).
Boss
🌿 Quagmire reuse — Reimagined for the fen — the bog floods and reed tiles respawn at 1.5×, but the surge only comes at high tide: ride each high tide to out-harvest the mire before low tide strands you short of the target (respawn_boost × the tide cycle).
Alt idea: The Fenmother — a colossal heron-witch who steals a column into the fog each turn unless fed a fish chain.
Tile set — palette & signature pieces
  • Stilt platform + pylons — The buildable hummock — a planked deck on driven pylons over water.
  • Boardwalk (autotile) — Raised plank walkway with straight / corner / T / branch / rail pieces.
  • Lily-pad & cattail clusters — Open-water dressing; firefly perches at dusk.
  • Heron Gate beacon — Staged landmark — reed shrine → cistern fountain → bog-lantern → heron arch.

New resources — and where they come from (no softlocks)
Obsidian — mined from cooled lava at the frontier (a board resource, not a craft good) — the zone's premium currency, used to gate the higher terraces
Signature mechanic

A rising heat meter and live lava channels you physically route mining around. Each new terrace is cooled lava you cleared at the frontier — the mountain hands you the next shelf to build on — and obsidian, the premium currency, is only won by clearing that cooled lava.

Hazards — reused from the game + new
reuse Lava flow — A molten tongue spreads to a random adjacent cell each turn, destroying resources in its path. Counter: No direct clear — route your mining away from the channel; obsidian dikes (built) pin its direction.
reuse Ash cloud — Falling ash dims a region of the board; dimmed tiles read face-grey and can't be targeted until cleared around. Counter: Chain at the cloud's edge to thin it; the watchtower shortens its duration.
new Ember rain — Cinders ignite 1–2 random tiles per turn (fire rules); fire spreads to neighbours if left. Counter: Chain burning tiles to snuff them (coins per tile), as with wildfire.
new Tremor — A quake jolts the board: one row shuffles its tiles sideways, scrambling near-complete chains. Counter: The observatory predicts the tremor row a turn ahead so you can cash chains early.
Boss
🔥 Ember Drake reuse — Reimagined on the slope — a heat tile spawns each turn and pools on the LOWEST terrace first, so build high and mine low: anything left on heat 2+ turns burns away. Land 3 iron bars before the heat climbs the whole mountain (heat_tiles × the terraces).
Alt idea: Magmaw — a lava-worm that surfaces through a terrace, turning a 2×2 block molten until quenched by a stone chain.
Tile set — palette & signature pieces
  • Terrace shelf + retaining wall — The buildable cooled-obsidian step cut into the slope, with a basalt retaining edge.
  • Switchback road (autotile) — Hairpin road pieces: straight, hairpin-left, hairpin-right, ramp-up.
  • Lava channel (animated) — Flowing molten surface with crust crackle; the live hazard + obsidian frontier.
  • Magma Crown furnace — Staged landmark — brazier → tap-furnace → great forge → caldera crown.

New resources — and where they come from (no softlocks)
Hide & amber — board resources — hide from the highland herds, amber foraged from the glacier ice — so they're producible before the rung that spends them
Signature mechanic

Warmth is a resource: the spring projects a warmth radius and tiles outside it freeze faster. You expand the hold by extending bonfires — drop one beside a frozen column to thaw it within a turn. Heat, not space, is the real frontier against the snow.

Hazards — reused from the game + new
reuse Hard freeze — Two columns freeze solid and can't be chained until thawed — worse the farther from the spring (freeze rules). Counter: Drop a bonfire on the board edge nearest a frozen column; anything inside its warmth radius thaws within a turn. The hearth keeps the central columns permanently warm.
new Blizzard whiteout — A whiteout hides a patch of tiles face-blank for a few turns; you chain blind. Counter: Tiles inside the warmth radius stay visible; the watchtower narrows the whiteout.
reuse Ice spike — Spikes erupt and block 3–4 board cells like rubble. Counter: Clear via an adjacent grain/log chain (warm produce shatters the ice).
new Avalanche — Snow sweeps a full row from the top edge downward, carrying tiles off unless braced. Counter: Watchtower + windbreak walls (built) brace the threatened row; chain it out before the sweep lands.
Boss
❄️ Frostmaw reuse — Reimagined around the warmth — columns freeze from the OUTSIDE in (farthest from the spring first), so the warm heart is your safe zone; thaw outward with bonfires and harvest 30 oak logs before the ice closes over the spring itself (freeze_columns × warmth).
Alt idea: The Wendigo — a starving frost-spirit that snuffs your outermost bonfire each turn, shrinking the warm zone.
Tile set — palette & signature pieces
  • Thawed ground patch — The buildable cleared ground — warm mud ringed by snowmelt within the spring's reach.
  • Windbreak palisade (autotile) — Snow-laden log wall sheltering the outer rings; straight / corner / gate pieces.
  • Steaming hot-spring — Animated turquoise spring with rising steam — the warm heart and central landmark base.
  • Bonfire (warmth emitter) — Built prop that projects a thaw radius; flickering light, melt-ring underneath.

Signature mechanic

Two live systems pull against each other: bank stockpile into caravans for big coin multipliers when a route is open, while mirage tiles (the hazard) disguise one resource as another — so the safest big chain and the most profitable one are rarely the same tiles. Greed vs. certainty, every turn.

Hazards — reused from the game + new
new Sandstorm — A wall of dust sweeps across the board over 2–3 turns, burying tiles it passes (face-sand) and relocating a few. Counter: Walls (built) on the windward side break the storm; chain ahead of the front to bank resources.
new Drought — The spring runs low: yields drop and your stockpile drains faster until water is restored. Counter: Chain water/oasis tiles to refill the cistern; irrigation buildings soften the penalty.
new Scorpion swarm — Scorpions surface in a cluster and creep outward, stinging (locking) tiles they cover. Counter: A 4+ chain over the cluster scatters them for coins (rat/locust rules).
new Quicksand — Patches turn sticky — tiles on them take two chains to clear, eating turns. Counter: Plank causeways (built) firm up routes; a tool can solidify a patch.
new Mirage — Heat-shimmer disguises a cluster of tiles as the wrong resource; a chain built on a misread mirage fails and wastes the turn. Counter: Chain a single tile at the mirage's edge to dispel the shimmer and reveal the true tiles before you commit a big chain.
Boss
🐉 The Sandwyrm new — The wyrm tunnels beneath the board: each turn it surfaces and buries a moving 2×3 block (rubble that drifts). Mine the target before it swallows the oasis.
Alt idea: The Dust Djinn — swaps two random tiles each turn, sabotaging set-ups; banished by a long single-colour chain.
Tile set — palette & signature pieces
  • Ring wall + gate (autotile) — The concentric oasis wall — straight / corner / gate / tower pieces that wrap each new ring.
  • Caravan road spoke — Packed-sand road radiating from the gate into the dunes, with wheel-rut detail.
  • Date-palm & oasis pool — Animated palm sway over the turquoise spring — the green heart in the gold.
  • Sunspire gnomon — Staged landmark — wellhead → sundial spire → grand sundial → the brass Sunspire.

New resources — and where they come from (no softlocks)
Pearls — a board resource (oyster tiles on the fishing board), producible from tier 0 — so the rungs that spend it never softlock
Signature mechanic

A vertical logistics puzzle no flat zone has: the cargo lift physically carries resource tiles between shelves, so where you mine (low) and where you craft (high) sit on different levels — and the harbour rows at the base flood and drain with the tide. You play up and down, not just across.

Hazards — reused from the game + new
reuse Squall (min-chain) — In rough weather, fish chains under length 4 fail and waste the turn (storm rules). Counter: The storm-watch flags the squall a turn early; bank short chains before it hits.
new Riptide — The undertow drags the bottom harbour row one cell seaward each turn; tiles lost off the edge are gone. Counter: Chain the bottom row to land the catch before it's pulled out; the harbour wall (built) slows the drag.
new Rogue wave — Every few turns a wave floods the lowest two rows, washing loose tiles away. Counter: The lighthouse predicts the wave; a sea-wall building shields one row.
new Gull raid — Gulls dive on fish/egg tiles and carry them off, like wolves but from above and faster. Counter: A scare-chain (any 3 adjacent) near the flock drives them off for a turn.
Boss
🌩 The Storm reuse — Reimagined with the beacon — the squall demands fish chains of 4+, but the lighthouse sweep briefly calms ONE column each pass, so you time your big chains to the rotating light. Land 6 fillets before the window closes (min_chain × the beacon sweep).
Alt idea: The Kraken — a tentacle rises each turn and yanks a column down into the sea unless a pearl chain severs it.
Tile set — palette & signature pieces
  • Cliff terrace shelf — The buildable carved shelf with a safety rail and a stair landing.
  • Switchback stair + cargo lift — Vertical connectors between shelves; the lift is the tier-gated growth piece.
  • Tide-pool harbour (animated) — Rock pools and surf at the cliff base that flood/drain with the tide — the fishing board.
  • Sweeping beacon — Staged landmark — watch-lantern → stone beacon → lighthouse → great headland beacon.

Signature mechanic

You grow UP, not out: each rung climbs to a higher layer — understory → mid-canopy → emergent crown — and roaming sun-shafts super-charge any plant tile they cross. The brighter (higher) you build, the richer the harvest, so the town reaches for the light.

Hazards — reused from the game + new
reuse Spore bloom — Fungus spreads across the platforms, fouling tiles it covers (fungus rules). Counter: Chain through the spread to cut it back; the herbalist halves the spread rate.
reuse Canopy prowlers — Big cats stalk the bird/egg tiles along the bridges, eating them each turn (wolf rules, reflavoured). Counter: A scare-chain or a built watchtower drives them off (scared state).
new Thornvine overgrowth — Vines creep back over cleared tiles, blocking them; they advance like a slow fire if ignored. Counter: Chain the vine tiles to cut them (coins per cut); the sawmill keeps adjacent boughs clear.
new Beetle infestation — Wood-borers cluster on a platform and chew plank/tree tiles outward. Counter: A 4+ chain over the cluster smokes them out (rat/locust rules).
Boss
🌱 Mossback reuse — Reimagined in the canopy — four tiles hide face-down under moss, but a roaming sun-shaft reveals any hidden tile it crosses, so you chase the light to expose the moss and gather 30 berries before the canopy swallows them (hide_resources × the sun-shaft).
Alt idea: The Elder Bramble — a waking treant that ensnares a bridge each turn, cutting one platform off until freed by an axe chain.
Tile set — palette & signature pieces

The craft bar — what every zone is reviewed against

These six principles, inherited from the town-layout foundation, are non-negotiable. A zone build that breaks one fails review and gets iterated. They are the “same quality bar” the brief asks for.

Roads first, buildings front the street

Lay the route network, then place each building beside a route at a fixed setback, facing it. A road is never drawn TO a building's centre — that is the #1 incoherence tell. Roads run between buildings.

The wilderness is the progress bar

Every zone is a clearing pushed into something — forest, ash, ice, dune, fog, void. The receding frontier IS the visible growth. It only ever recedes; cleared ground never re-wilds.

A bounded landmark that levels up in place

One fixed focal hub the buildings ring. It never grows over its neighbours; only its landmark sprite transforms with the town (well → fountain → monument; campfire → forge → magma crown).

Stable, additive growth

Tier N keeps every lot, road and prop of tier N−1 and only APPENDS. A placed building never moves when the town grows. Each upgrade is an earned, legible before/after — a milestone, not a counter tick.

Kill grid-death

Vary lot footprints, jitter placement 5–15%, curve the spine, stagger frontage. A uniform lattice reads as placeholder. Density and ambient life (NPC routines, props, particles) rise each rung.

Distinct identity per rung

Each tier introduces a new landmark, a path-material upgrade (dirt → cobble → stone), and a visible jump in life. The skyline changes. The player should be able to name the era at a glance.

From proposal to playable — the build-out plan

This atlas is Pass 1. Each zone then becomes its own interactive doc with a live top-down Grow play-through on the real 40×30 @ 32px game grid, a tile inventory (the art backlog), and an art bible — exactly like town-layout — then is critically reviewed against the craft bar, iterated to a high bar, and merged. Advancements found building one zone are back-ported to the others (the shared engine that draws these cards is the cross-pollination mechanism: improve it once, every zone benefits).
