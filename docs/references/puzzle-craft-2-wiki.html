01Introduction & Game Overview Reviewed

Puzzle Craft 2 is a puzzle town-building game and the sequel to the original Puzzle Craft.

Mine, craft, build!

Match tiles, collect valuable resources, craft tools and build up your towns.

Influence & magical runes

Use your influence and magical runes to extend seasons, find new species, summon villagers, and more.

Land, air and sea

Discover new plants, animals and resources. Venture out to sea for supplies and long-lost treasures.

Rule them all!

Travel a massive kingdom, rule many settlements, and develop a mighty empire — the biggest Puzzle Craft yet.

Gold Stars on a tile indicate Bonus Experience is awarded when it is collected. Bonus XP for special puzzles scales with the number of friends in your Embassy (Friends List), plus extra from finishing rare collections. Amounts listed here are the base (minimum). Example: a 100% bonus on a base 5 XP puzzle rewards 10 XP; a 200% bonus rewards 15 XP.
Platforms (v1.5, released May 18 2016): iOS — App Store; Android — Google Play; PC — Windows Store (Windows 8.1/10, app ID 9nblggh67l90).

⚠ Flagged in this pass — verify before trusting.
  • Worker count vs trophy. The Workers tables list 64 workers (Farm 21 + Mine 21 + Sea 22), but the Bronze trophy reads “Hire 63 workers.” Sea has one extra “find a coin” worker (Buccaneer + Pirate + Castaway) where Farm/Mine have two. One side is off by one.
  • 31,337 influence. The Platinum trophy target “Reach at least 31,337 influence” is 31337 = “eleet” leetspeak. Could be a genuine dev easter-egg value or an invented one — confirm against the wiki.
  • Surf trophy / decoration mismatch. The Decorations table ties Surfer Camp to the “Cowabunga” trophy, while the Bronze list says “Ride the wave (tap the umbrella decoration).” Same theme, two different names — likely a conflation.
  • Mine price inversion. In Resources, Rubble (12/120) out-sells both Stone (10/80) and Iron (11/100) despite rubble being lower-tier debris. Plausible but counter-intuitive — worth a second look.
  • Worker terminology drift. Sea worker Artisan reads “1 clam less to gather pearls” while neighbours (Diver, Buccaneer) say oyster. Cosmetic, but inconsistent.
  • Outlier worker bonuses. Peasant (8 grass) and Digger (4 dirt) reduce by large amounts where every other worker reduces by 1. Plausible (grass/dirt gather in bulk) but unverified.

10Tips, Tricks & Advanced Strategies Reviewed

Beginner fundamentals followed by community-sourced grinding loops (Darin Kaplan, Nathaniel Stapleton, et al.).

Getting started fundamentals · Gamezebo

Pick one goal

Focus each session on a single target — a building, a challenge, or a tile to unlock. Chasing several at once litters the board with mixed tiles and makes long chains harder.

Choose your active tile

Each category has several types (Chicken / Turkey / Pheasant…), each with a strength. Set the active one between boards to match your goal — e.g. Chickens for eggs, Turkeys for herd animals.

Read the exact tile cost

Activate a tile, open the Workers menu, tap the related worker — its icon shows tiles-per-item (e.g. Poultryman: 9 turkeys vs 7 chickens per egg). On the board, tap a tile for its normal-item count, tap-and-hold for the long-chain bonus count.

Craft tools before you enter

Crafting is far cheaper than buying. A shovel costs ~1 wood to craft vs 40 gold to buy mid-board if you run out. Revisit completed towns to craft from their stockpiles (you can craft any tool you know) and replay their boards for cheaper resources.

Free moves stack

Many tiles/buildings grant moves that don’t cost a turn. They add up — Turkeys (2) + Small Animal Pen (3) = 5 free bird moves — and apply to multi-collection too. The only way to see how many remain is the pop-up at the top of the board, so watch it.

Collect building & friend gifts

Buildings dispense tools on timers — tap them often, but drop below max first or the overflow is wasted. Embassy friend gifts are free to send (3 messengers/24h, respawn every 8h), can include magic tools, and exceed the tool cap (6/5).

The long-chain & research mechanic why workers matter

Unlocking a new species needs a number of long chains — a “long chain” is however many tiles produce that tile’s bonus item (default 10). Workers that cut the tiles-needed (say 10→8) cut the long-chain requirement too, so species get easier to unlock as you hire. After unlocking, a tile must be researched (magnifying-glass phase) before it receives full worker bonuses. Hire to the per-job cap whenever you can — workers are permanent across towns — and spend runes at the Magic Portal to call workers (3 runes each, one of the best rune uses).

Moving to the next town don’t lose progress

You advance by building the structure marked with a covered wagon + green arrow. When you move on you pack your gold, tools, workers and level — but leave all resources behind and lose EXP banked toward the next level, so level up first if you’re close. Three en-route challenges follow (no tile control, no resources kept). Build everything before leaving: skipped buildings remain available later but at a much higher level requirement.

On the sea tactics

Place islands where you want them

An island/fish spawns on the last water tile of the chain that forms it — so end chains near your ship and near each other.

Chests first; use every move

Treasure chests give 1–3 finished items — prioritise them even if you abandon islands. Use all moves to bank salt/water; you only need to touch an island/fish/chest, so move through it to water.

Mine hazards & chaining tactics

Countdown tiles tick on tool use

Rats, lava and hidden runes count down even when you use a tool (tools don’t cost a move, but they do advance these). A single gas tile can be tapped to clear it for one turn if you’re out of lamps.

Lava & the corner turn

Clear a lava tile’s whole column below it to drop it straight to the bottom row instead of letting it drip and ruin rocks. And turn at diagonals — corners let one chain reach tiles that look unreachable.

Economy & XP. Build the Witch’s Hut to watch short ads (when the blue ! shows) for 1 rune / 30–80 coins / 10–20 influence, ~20/day. Spend gold on playing boards, not buying resources outright (a single run usually recoups its cost). The biggest XP source is challenges — villager quests and the always-available pair — not raw chaining. Watch for bouncing menu icons (rewards waiting). It’s a long game by design — don’t rush.

Fundamentals, town-move, sea/mine tactics and economy notes above are from the Gamezebo guide (Jillian Werner).

1 · Darin Kaplan’s Rune Farming Loop

First village (lower-left), farm. Setup: Fruit→Lemon, Bird→Chameleon. Clear the board with tools, not moves until only trees & grass remain → Sapling turns grass into trees → Golden Apple turns all trees into Lemons → one long chain collects all 36 lemons for many coins → convert coins to runes. Loop: remove stray species with tools (never draw — that resets the Chameleon bonus), then Trimmer → Fertilizer → Rake → Bird Feed to fill the board with Chameleons; collect them for runes (~5 Chameleons = 1 rune). Up to 100+ runes/season.

2 · Stapleton’s Cheaper Rune Loop (~50/season, no magic tools)

4th village. Need Onion, Lemon, and any grain except buckwheat. Clear to all grass → Fertilizer + Rake to make all grain → Fertilizer again, collect grain → ~20 Onions → 20 Lemons → coins → rune, then loop. Uses 2 extra Fertilizers but no Magic Portal tools.

3 · Gold Farming

Method 1 — Hen/Peccary/Alpaca

4th village. Clear to grass → Fertilizer → Bird Feed → 36 Hens → 36 Peccary → 36 Alpaca → 18 coins = ~900 gold per chain. ~6k gold/season plus runes.

Method 2 — Treasure maps

Buy Legendary + Mythic maps & spyglasses (5 total). Use at the 4th village, sell goods at max price. ~10k of goods per run.

12Frequently Asked Questions Reviewed

How can I find fish in the sea?

Two steps: (1) connect Deep Water tiles — a navigation move; the number of Deep Water tiles needed per fish depends on how many Fisherman workers you’ve hired. (2) Move your ship onto the fish — a ship move. Earn fish in a long enough chain and you get an octopus instead (scaled by Trawlerman workers).

Android: how do I enable / delete Cloud Save?

Enable: Main menu → Options → Options again → “Sign in” (bottom) → “Sign in” again → configure your Google Play Games profile → check “Cloud Save.”
Delete: turn off Cloud Save and sign out → open Google Drive → Settings → Managing Apps → Puzzle Craft 2 → “Delete Hidden app data” → wait ~1 hour, then sign back in.

Is the game available on PC?

Yes — in the Windows Store (Puzzle Craft 2, app ID 9nblggh67l90). Remote support is available via the ATGames support tool.

13Currencies & Glossary Reviewed

Coins

The primary currency — used to buy tools, resources and certain puzzles. Collectible on all boards, mostly from long chains (e.g. vegetables when no fruit is meant to be discovered).

Runes

Premium currency — used to buy coins, get extra townsfolk, and buy certain puzzle types. Spawn randomly on all boards, most commonly in the mine.

Influence

Earned from watching an ad, completing quests, and other means. Spent on decorations (shrubs, trees, flowers, …).

  • Puzzle / Puzzle piece — any tile type on a board.
  • Navigation Move — dragging a path to connect matching tiles (farm, mine, or sea).
  • Ship Move — on the sea board, explicitly directing the ship’s movement to collect earned tiles.
  • Long chain — a chain long enough to procure the “next tier” species or special bonus.
  • Worker Credit — required (plus resources) to hire a worker; earned from houses or runes.
  • Embassy — your Friends List; more friends raise special-puzzle bonus XP.
