Town Layout — Growing Outpost → City

A fresh, organic home-town layout that starts as a tiny forest outpost and visibly grows into a city — wilderness clears into plots, a central landmark evolves, paths upgrade, and the place feels more alive at every rung. The farm board (the core puzzle) is a plot-sized widget at the heart of town, so it's always a tap away. This is a layout mockup (flat colors, no pixel art yet) drawn on the real 40×30 @ 32px grid so coordinates drop straight into the game.

How to read growth. The forest edge (the tree-line ring) is the progress bar: each rung the developed clearing expands and the woods recede toward the frame. Empty cleared pads are where the player drops buildings. The farm board sits beside the landmark at the very centre — the core loop is always one tap from the plaza. The landmark goes well → fountain → town-square monument; the paths out of the plaza upgrade dirt → cobble.

Why redo it

Today the home town (src/ui/town/townMaps.ts) is a placeholder rectangular grid: flat grass, two straight dirt bands, a tiny plaza disc, and building plots dropped onto a rigid HOME_COLS × HOME_ROWS lattice — with the farm board parked as a giant slab on the right third. There is no forest, no wilderness, no curves, no varied lots, and no sense of growth — a tier upgrade just adds more identical cells. It reads as unfinished.

The goal is the opposite: a place that feels like a cheerful little settlement growing up around you, with the farm puzzle right at its heart, where every upgrade is a visible, earned milestone.

Design principles

Organic radial growth. Plots ring out from the central plaza in loose, varied clusters — not a lattice. Slight jitter + mixed lot sizes make it read hand-placed. (Townscaper, Dorfromantik.)
The puzzle is the heart. The farm board is a plot-sized widget right next to the landmark at town centre — front-and-centre and always a tap away, since it's the core loop. The town grows in a full ring around it.
One evolving landmark. A single focal point anchors the scene and levels up with the town: well → fountain+plaza → town-square monument. It never moves. (Pelican Town square, Animal Crossing plazas.)
Wilderness → plots = the progress bar. The town is carved out of forest; each rung clears a wider ring. Receding woods are the clearest "we grew" signal, no UI text needed. (Ooblets clearing, Ashes of Creation stages.)
Path material as a free stage cue. Dirt tracks (outpost) → cobble near the plaza (village) → mostly cobbled streets (city). The ground itself tells you how far you've come.
Avoid "grid death." Uniform spacing on a straight grid is what reads as placeholder. The fixes are cheap: a focal centre, varied lot sizes, jitter, edge vegetation, rising prop & NPC density.
Additive & stable. "The town grows on top of the previous level." We keep the engine's existing contract: lots.length === tiers[tier].plots, and a lot's index is stable across rungs (tier N keeps N−1's indices and only appends). So the map grows exactly like the town — nothing already placed ever shifts.

  2. Insert the Outpost rung in src/features/cartography/data.ts, redistribute unlocks, bump the save schema, move the quarry gate.
  3. Extend src/ui/town/TownScene.ts ground painting for forest / cobble / cleared-pad terrain + transition tiles, and re-anchor the farm board at the town centre.
  4. Generate the tile/prop art via the PixelLab MCP (the pixellab / seasonal-tile-pipeline skills).
