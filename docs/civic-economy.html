Town Hall — Tithes & Provisions

A return-to-collect civic economy: your settlements pay coin taxes (income everywhere) and provision free tools (from your most-progressed town), both gated by a real-clock cooldown — with a board-native twist that turns "collecting" into a move you actually play.

Overview

Puzzle Craft 2's kingdom pays the player free tools and taxes over time based on the buildings they own. We adopt that loop because it fills a real gap: today, the only payoff for building up a town is a one-time season_bonus/grant_tool at season-end — there is no reason to return between sessions and no passive economy. The Town Hall gives the town you grew a way to work for you.

TL;DR. Visit the Town Hall, wait out an 8-hour cooldown, and Collect. You instantly receive coin tax (summed across every founded settlement) and a queued bundle of free provisions (tools, set by your strongest town's buildings). The provisions don't drop into your bag — they arrive as a care-package crate on your next board that you tap to open.

Our twist over Puzzle Craft 2

PC2 collection is a tap off a menu. Ours is the same source of supply, but the delivery is a board move: matching the world is the core verb of this game, so claiming should happen on the board, not in a list. The crate is a one-tile gift that can't form a chain — you tap it to open it, it bursts, and the queued tools land in your bag.

Puzzle Craft 2

Buildings drip tools + taxes on a timer. Collect by tapping each building in a menu. Purely off-board bookkeeping.

puzzleDrag2 (ours)

Same building-driven supply, but provisions are delivered as a care-package crate seeded onto your next board — collecting is a tactile, on-board moment that reinforces the drag-match loop.

Why discrete + dividend. We chose a discrete cooldown claim (a fixed bundle each visit) over a continuous idle drip — simpler to read and balance, with a clear "come back" cadence — and taxes are a pure coin dividend (upside only, no upkeep/penalty), so the system never feels punitive. Future tension levers (overflow decay, keeper-allegiance tool-vs-coin split, seasonal modulation) are catalogued under Deferred.

What drives the numbers

QuantityScope ("where")Derived from
Tax (coins)Every founded settlement — income everywherebase + perBuilding × buildingCount, summed
Provisions (free tools)The single most-progressed townIts built buildings, mapped via CIVIC_PROVISIONS

Both are computed live at claim time from state.built / state.settlements, so they always reflect the current town — nothing to keep in sync. "Most-progressed" = highest settlementTier, tie-broken by building count.

The claim → crate → grant loop

Why only on initial fills. The crate seeds during board regeneration, never during a mid-board collapse/refill. Opening the crate triggers a collapseBoard(); gating on initial guarantees a second crate can't pop in before React clears carePackagePending.

State & actions

// GameState.civicEconomy  (src/types/state.ts)
civicEconomy: {
  lastClaimedAt: number | null;            // wall-clock ms of last collection
  pendingProvisions: Record<string, number> // tools awaiting board pickup
}
ActionOwnerEffect
CIVIC/CLAIMsliceIf cooldown elapsed: coins += taxYield, merge provisionsRoster into pendingProvisions, set lastClaimedAt, bump _boardNonce (only when provisions queued). Else: no-op (same ref).
CIVIC/OPEN_CARE_PACKAGEsliceDeposit pendingProvisions into state.tools, clear the queue. No-op (same ref) when nothing queued.
Slice registration. Both actions are owned exclusively by the slice, so they're listed in SLICE_PRIMARY_ACTIONS (the documented footgun) and the slice is added to the slices array in src/state.ts. Referential equality is preserved for every rejected/no-op path.

Offline accrual & persistence

Because the gate compares Date.now() against a stored lastClaimedAt timestamp (rather than the daily-streak local-date string), the cooldown ticks while the app is closed — reopen after 8h and the claim is ready. Old saves are migrated forward (schema v49→v50) by seeding a fresh, claimable civicEconomy slice.
