Tide Lanes

The direct translation of Puzzle Craft 2 water board into our chain verb. Drag a chain of water to carve a LANE; your boat sails it, hauling in every fish and clam it passes, and a fresh catch SPAWNS where your lane ends — so you decide where the next fish appears. Navigate, then harvest by reaching.

Event log

How to play

  • Drag a lane across 3+ touching water tiles of the same depth — all deep, or all shallow (8 directions). Fish, clams and the boat block the lane.
  • On release the lane is consumed (deep → +Water, shallow → +Salt) and your boat ⛵ sails to the last cell of the lane.
  • Haul: every 🐟 / 🐚 touching any lane cell is reeled in (+Fish / +Shells). Harvest by reaching — long lanes that brush past catches collect more.
  • You place the next spawn: a fresh catch appears on open sea right next to where your lane ended. End your lane near where you want fish to gather.
  • Consumed water cascades down and the top refills from open sea; catches stay put on the surface.

Why it is ours: Puzzle Craft 2 expanded a near-identical drag-match game by adding the SEA: a boat you steer and spawns you place. This prototype ports exactly that lever — a mover on the board + player-directed spawn placement + harvest-by-reaching — onto our chain verb. It is the most direct answer to "what can a water board add to ours".
