Event log

How to play

  • Swipe across the board in any of four directions — or tap an ↑ ↓ ← → arrow. The whole field tilts.
  • Every tile slides as far as it can toward that edge (like 2048, but tiles never merge). The trailing edge then refills with fresh tiles, keeping the field dense (but never fully packed — there is always slack to slide).
  • Any line of 3+ same-family tiles (a row or column run) auto-clears. Then the board falls in the same direction and re-matches — a cascade. Each cascade step bumps the combo multiplier.
  • Score = tiles cleared × the combo multiplier of the step that cleared them. One smart gesture can detonate the whole board.

Why it is ours: It reinterprets the core input itself: the drag gesture stops selecting tiles and starts controlling board physics. It is the boldest end of the spectrum — a "what if the board moved" reframing — yet still resolves into our familiar 3+ same-family matching and cascades.
