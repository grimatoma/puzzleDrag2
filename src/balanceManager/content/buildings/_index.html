Buildings are permanent structures you construct in a settlement. Each building costs raw resources to build, and many grant an ongoing ability — the [[buildings:mill]] reduces a bread recipe's flour cost, the [[buildings:granary]] raises your inventory cap and adds a turn, the [[buildings:bakery]] unlocks the full baked-goods menu, and the [[buildings:forge]] opens the high-value metal-smithing tier. Buildings unlock at settlement levels and some require that another building be present first.

Once built, a building is available for the lifetime of that settlement. Crafting stations (Bakery, Kitchen, Workshop, Forge, Larder, Smokehouse) appear here in the catalog and also drive the [[recipes:rec_bread|recipes]] system — you must have the station built before you can craft its recipes (instantly, when you have the inputs). Purely cosmetic buildings (Inn, Clock Tower) count toward town-planning achievements without occupying a recipe slot; other civic buildings such as the [[buildings:chapel|Chapel]] and [[buildings:observatory|Observatory]] are not crafting stations either, but they do carry passive abilities (a season coin bonus, a gem-threshold reducer) shown on their own pages. Beyond crafting, buildings shape the board itself. A category's producer building acts as a spawner — raising it puts that whole tile family into the [[systems:spawn-pool|spawn pool]] — so your construction choices decide what you can [[systems:chaining|chain]]. Plots are scarce, so the loadout is a genuine decision: you can demolish a building to free its plot and remove its power or category, then build something else. Civic buildings feed a small passive coin trickle, and cosmetic decorations raise a settlement's influence without taking a recipe slot.
