Seasons divide a play session into four equal stretches. A farm session runs a fixed turn budget — a home run is turns — and that budget is split into Spring, Summer, Autumn and Winter in turn. The old calendar-year season system was retired; what remains is this in-session quartering plus the seasonal artwork on the tiles.

Each season biases the [[systems:spawn-pool|spawn pool]] toward different tile categories, so the board you play in Spring is not the board you play in Winter — and a sharp player lines up the categories they want with the season that favours them. When the turn budget runs out the session closes and is scored: your settlement progress and [[systems:inventory|stored goods]] persist, but the board itself resets for the next run.
