Recipes are how raw resources become refined goods. Each recipe names a crafting station (a building such as the Bakery, Mill, Workshop, Forge, or larder), a set of resource inputs, and the single item it produces. You craft a recipe at its station once you have its inputs in inventory — and once you have built the station.

Recipes are the middle of the supply chain: the board credits raw resources (wheat becomes flour, birds give eggs, ore becomes iron bars), recipes combine those into goods worth more coins or usable as expedition food, and those goods then pay for the next building or tier-up. The keystone example is the bread recipe — flour plus eggs become bread at the Bakery (listed below).

Some buildings modify recipes: building the Mill reduces the flour the bread recipe needs by one. Tools are crafted at the Workshop the same way. Ore-to-bar conversion happens automatically through the chain pipeline rather than as a recipe; recipes then turn bars into forged goods.
