Workers are hires that quietly bend the rules in your favour. Each worker has a type (the effect it grants) and a tier (how strong that effect is); you hire them with coins and resources, and a settlement's housing capacity limits how many you can keep at once — so the cost ramp and the housing cap are real choices.

Their bonuses are passive and they stack: hiring a [[workers:farmer|Farmer]] trims a tile off the grain chain (and a [[workers:miner|Miner]] off stone), so those families [[systems:promotion|promote]] sooner; a [[workers:baker|Baker]] shaves flour off the bread recipe, cutting [[systems:crafting|crafting]] costs; and coin- and rune-focused hires pad what you earn from [[systems:trade|trade]]. Together they let you tune a settlement toward whichever loop you're leaning on.
