What the game is

A cozy match-merge farming & settlement game. On a small board you drag chains of matching tiles; each chain harvests goods and merges tiles into better ones (see [[systems:chaining]] and [[systems:promotion]]). You spend what you grow to build up a settlement, then outfit a caravan and roll out to found new towns across the map — opening and developing several at your own pace. Each town stacks a new board on the last and grows bigger and harder, but the heart never changes: drag, harvest, grow, complete, move on.

The fantasy. You arrive at a cold hearth in a forgotten vale ([[zones:home]]) and someone hands you the tongs. You re-light the farm, build a caravan, and head out across the map — a river valley, a volcano's cooled terraces, a sea-cliff harbour ([[zones:harbor]]), a rat-plagued meadow ([[zones:meadow]]), a deep crystal delve ([[zones:caves]]) — until you raise the capital and wake the kingdom ([[zones:oldcapital]]).

Design pillars

PillarWhat it means
One compounding loopEvery system feeds a single flywheel: play the board → harvest → spend on growth → the board yields more. Depth comes from stacking on that loop, never a second parallel game.
Cozy early, ramping lateThe first sites are forgiving teachers. Hazards and bosses arrive only once the player has the tools to meet them. Effort-gated, never time-gated.
Choose your buildPlots are scarce, so which buildings, workers and tools you raise is a real decision — and you can demolish to re-spec. The puzzle math stays fixed; you change what the board hands you.
A world that growsThe map is the progression bar. Each settlement visibly expands from outpost to city in place, and the frontier of foundable towns unfurls outward as you go.

The core loop

Play the board → harvest → spend on growth → bigger yield → back to play. One chain costs one turn; longer chains yield more goods and merge tiles up their family ladder. Raw goods refine at station buildings (grain → [[resources:flour]] at the [[buildings:mill]] → [[resources:bread]] at the [[buildings:bakery]]); the refined economy is spent to grow the town, which makes the next round bigger. The same four beats repeat in every town, at every scale.

Boards build on each other

What keeps it from being "the same board over and over": a new board type is always fed by the boards before it. The farm makes food; food buys mine expeditions; farm food (bait) plus mine metal (tackle) fund the fishery. So every town with a mine also has a farm, and every fishery town has a farm and a mine. This feed-dependency is also the game's main soft gate. The one deliberate exception is a deep mine with no farm, where provisioning under scarcity is the whole point.

The shape of a session & the long arc

A session is one visit to a board: a small budget of turns (home runs base), each chain spends one, whatever you harvest banks into that town's stockpile. Each town is a long project, not a handful of sessions — but it is effort-gated, not time-gated. The time between sessions hands you free tools (timed depots, daily, civic) to go faster — or you craft those tools now by spending extra materials.

The long arc is a map of settlements — a required spine the finale needs, plus optional frontier towns for content and cross-zone bonuses. You found each by caravan and need not finish one before opening the next. Workers, coins, tools and Villagers travel with you; each town's materials are its own. The full map and gate model are on the Progression page; the per-town detail is in the Settlement Atlas.

What is — and isn't — in scope

In scopeOut of scope (for now)
One farm, one mine, one fish board — stacked, never parallel mini-games. A required town spine + optional frontier. Per-town tier ladders, three currencies, crafting, workers, tools, expeditions, hazards & bosses, boons. The always-on meta wrap and a free-to-play money model. A narrative capstone finale. Multiplayer / social. A second core game bolted on. Per-town economies that don't feed the loop. Deep balance of the late arc (specced but provisional). Permanent global power creep — cross-zone exports are bounded, named bonuses, not a stat-stacking tree.

"Fully spec the whole arc" means every settlement has a design; it does not mean every number is final. The early towns are tuning-locked; later towns carry provisional numbers — see Status.
