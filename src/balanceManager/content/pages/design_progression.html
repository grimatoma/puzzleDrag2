The founding model — non-linear, softly gated

Founding a town requires only that it is reachable on the map and that you can afford the rising caravan cost (a basket of goods that scales per founding). There is no "finish this town before founding the next." On top of that open base sit a few light, acyclic, pacing-oriented soft gates:

  • Board-feed dependency. You can't run a mine until you own a farm (mines eat food as expedition turns), nor a fish board until you own a farm + mine (bait + tackle). The same stacking shown in the Overview.
  • Low home-tier nudge. The first combination town asks a low home tier, so the farm loop is learned before the mine arrives — a nudge, not a wall.
  • Rising caravan cost. Each successive founding costs more goods, so the frontier unfurls outward over time. Coins fund session tolls and wages, not founding.
  • The finale gate (the only hard one): the capital opens once every required-spine town is complete.

The only dependencies are acyclic and feed-based. Caravan cost + map reach handle the rest of the pacing.

The no-softlock invariant

A gating resource must be producible by the rung before it is spent. Every tier-up and founding cost may only demand goods the player can already make — a board resource of a board they own, or a crafted good whose station unlocks earlier. This makes "non-linear + soft gates" safe, and the live progression oracle checks it. (The historical softlock — home once needed [[resources:bread|bread]] whose [[buildings:bakery|Bakery]] needed a mine good — was exactly a violation of this rule, since re-costed onto farm goods.)

What a tier-up does (every settlement)

  • Raises the plot count — more room for buildings, the core scarcity lever.
  • Unlocks building types — new stations, [[buildings:housing|houses]], and the town's signature export building.
  • Levels the landmark in place — the central structure visibly transforms, so growth reads at a glance.
  • Does not change the puzzle math — turn budget and yield are untouched; what changes is what you can build.

The finale

When every required-spine town is complete, the capital ([[zones:oldcapital]]) opens its capstone. The finale is narrative + payoff, not a new board type: a short run of story beats, a choice-reactive epilogue, and the build of a Monument that wakes the kingdom — plus a capstone reward and a place in the Chronicle. This supersedes the legacy "3 Hearth-Tokens" gate (kept as optional flavour). See the finale fiction on the Story page and the decision record on Status.
