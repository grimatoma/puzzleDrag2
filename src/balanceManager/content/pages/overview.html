What Hearthwood Vale is

Hearthwood Vale is a Phaser + React game that fuses a drag-to-chain puzzle board with a settlement-rebuilding loop. You are the descendant of a founder who, a century ago, banked the hearth of the Vale rather than letting it die — and swore one of their line would return. You are that one. The central verb is remember, not conquer: you rebuild a dormant kingdom one ruin at a time.

The core loop

A turn is one chain: drag through three or more adjacent matching tiles (8-direction movement, no tile reused). Long chains are intentional — at fixed thresholds along a chain, an upgraded tile spawns and a resource is credited. After each move the board collapses downward and refills.

  • Chain → resources + coins. One chain costs one turn; longer chains yield more. Resource yield is floor(chainLength / threshold) per the tile's UPGRADE_THRESHOLDS entry.
  • Refine & build. Raw goods feed buildings and recipes — grain becomes [[resources:flour]] at the [[buildings:mill]], flour becomes [[resources:bread]] at the [[buildings:bakery]].
  • Tier up & expand. Spend the refined economy to climb a settlement, then raise the wagon that wakes the next ruin.

Seasons & turns

A farm session is a fixed turn budget (home/[[zones:meadow]]/[[zones:quarry]] start at 10 turns; deeper zones at 12). That budget is split into four equal seasons (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter) that bias which tile categories spawn. The standalone calendar-year season system was removed; what remains is the in-session quartering plus the seasonal visuals.

Town & expeditions

Between boards you manage the town: build structures, craft at stations, fill orders for the townsfolk (Mira the baker, Bram the smith, and others), and travel the map to found new settlements. Bosses such as [[bosses:frostmaw]] appear as seasonal challenges.

Design pillars

  • Remember, not conquer. Progress is rediscovery of a dormant network of vows, not conquest.
  • One system at a time. Content reveals along the rebuild ladder; the first town teaches the farm, the mine arrives later.
  • The dignity of mundane keeping. Baking bread, naming a lane, tending a hearth — small acts carry weight.
  • Soft fail. No permanent setbacks; the only cost is time and one-shot consumables.
  • No pay-to-win. Premium currency only ever skips timers, never buys puzzle advantage.

How to use this wiki

This wiki is two things at once: a reference for the game as it exists today, and a drawing board for features that are partially built or planned.

  • Everything is generated live. Tile, resource, recipe, building, boss, and zone pages are built from the same source maps the game runs on (src/constants.ts, src/features/*/data.ts, the config schemas). If a value changes in code, the wiki changes with it — the catalog cannot drift.
  • Status flags mark built-vs-planned. Each concept and entity carries a status — WIRED, PARTIAL, STUB, DOC-ONLY, or PLANNED — so you always know whether something runs in normal play or is a future idea. See the Direction page for the full roadmap with status on every item.
  • Navigate by category, search, or cross-link. The sidebar groups concepts (Board, Economy, World, Systems, Screens). Follow the inline links — like [[resources:bread]] or [[buildings:bakery]] — to jump between related entities. Backlinks on each page show what points back.
  • This wiki is for game knowledge. Story, design, plans, upcoming-feature scope, and every config-generated concept live here. Code knowledge — how the project is built (catalog enums, the src/config/schemas/ definitions, feature-slice authoring) — lives in CLAUDE.md and alongside the code in src/, not in the wiki.

Read the Direction page for the committed build plan and settled calls, Balance baseline for the PC2-derived numbers we are matching, the Timeline for the live unlock feed, and Story for the world and characters. Anything under Parked / Future is deferred — not the current direction.
