The zone network is semi-linear by design: a tight teaching corridor at the start — two farm zones where everything is learned in order — then a widening web of specialist biomes that opens as you level. The game never leaves you guessing where to go next, but once the early gates lift you have genuine choices.

The shape of the journey

At level 1, your map shows exactly two reachable destinations: your home at [[zones:home|Hearthwood Vale]] and the neighbouring fields of [[zones:meadow|Greenmeadow]]. That is not an accident. The opening is a deliberate single-path teaching corridor — one board type, one economy, no distractions — so that the drag-chain, upgrade thresholds, orders, and the first buildings all land cleanly before anything else is asked of you.

Reaching level 2 cracks the map open. Two branches appear at once: [[zones:orchard|Wild Orchard]] to the south for richer farming, and [[zones:crossroads|The Crossroads]] leading onward to the first mine. From there the map fans out further with each level, through tides and festivals, into the deep stone, and finally to the boss arenas — until all three biomes have been mastered and the [[zones:oldcapital|Old Capital]] becomes reachable.

The world map as seen from the home hearth — Hearthwood Vale at the left, the rest of the network stretching to the right.

How travel opens up

A first visit to any zone needs two things at once: the zone must be adjacent to somewhere you have already been on the map, and your player level must meet or exceed the zone's level gate. Once you have visited a zone, fast-travel back is always free — adjacency and level only matter for the first unlock.

Three biome types gate the puzzle boards themselves, independently of zone adjacency:

  • Farm — always open. Every player begins on a farm board.
  • Mine — unlocks at level 2 (first available at [[zones:quarry|Cracked Quarry]]).
  • Harbor / fishing — unlocks at level 3 (first available at [[zones:harbor|Saltspray Harbor]]).

Think of the level gates as a pacing aid, not a wall: each new gate opens a qualitatively different experience — new board mechanics, new resource chains, new hazards — rather than just more of the same.

The phases

Phase 1 — The teaching corridor (Level 1)

[[zones:home|Hearthwood Vale]] and [[zones:meadow|Greenmeadow]] are the only two destinations available at level 1. This is the teaching corridor: a deliberately linear stretch where the drag-chain mechanic, upgrade thresholds, filling orders, and the first buildings all land cleanly before anything else is added to the picture. The tutorial runs here. You learn to chain hay into bundles, grain into flour, and trees into planks; you build the first mill, fill the granary, and learn what [[resources:supplies]] are for. Every mechanic that every later zone builds on is introduced here, unhurried.

The two zones share the same farm board and temperate tile pool — the repetition is the point. By the time you leave the corridor you should be thinking in chains, not clicking icons.

Phase 2 — Branching out (Level 2)

Level 2 breaks the corridor open into two branches you can pursue in either order.

The orchard branch — [[zones:orchard|Wild Orchard]] is another farm zone, but its tile mix skews toward fruits and trees rather than grain. Chains here are longer for a bigger payoff, and the upgrade map opens richer crop tiers. It is the place to deepen your farming before committing to the mine.

The mine branch — [[zones:crossroads|The Crossroads]] is a board-less waypoint (encounters, story fragments) that bridges the farm cluster to the mine cluster. Past it, [[zones:quarry|Cracked Quarry]] opens the mine economy: stone chains produce [[resources:block]], iron ore chains produce [[resources:iron_bar]], and coal produces [[resources:coke]]. Critically, mine turns are not free — you spend [[resources:supplies]] or [[resources:bread]] before each session to buy a turn budget. Running out of food ends the expedition. Hazards (cave-ins, gas vents, moles) add urgency that the farm never had.

Phase 3 — Tides & festivals (Level 3)

[[zones:harbor|Saltspray Harbor]] PARTIAL opens the third and final board type: the sea expedition. The harbor runs on a tide cycle — which fish tiles appear and at what rates shifts with the tide, adding a time-pressure rhythm absent from mine expeditions. Pearls ([[resources:pearls]]) are the signature output, used in high-value recipes and required (as a Tidesinger's Pearl Hearth-Token) to unlock the Old Capital.

[[zones:fairground|Drifter's Fairground]] opens at the same level gate as a board-less festival node — rotating limited-time offers, seasonal contests, and story fragments — bridging the mine cluster toward the deep zones.

Phase 4 — Into the deep (Levels 4–6)

The final playable zones are all mine-type, each demanding more than the last.

  • [[zones:caves|Lanternlit Caves]] (level 4) — a deeper mine with rare gem tiles and the lava hazard, which the [[zones:quarry|Quarry]] never sees. Lava tiles spread if left unchained; dealing with them while maintaining your ore output is the central skill of the Caves.
  • [[zones:forge|Black Forge]] (level 5) — the mastery mine, with the steepest entry cost and the richest output. Named for the smithy at its heart, the Forge is where boss-tier resources are smelted and where the most advanced crafted goods are made. Completing it earns the Pact-Iron Hearth-Token.
  • [[zones:pit|The Pit]] (level 6) PARTIAL — a board-less boss arena. No puzzle board; instead, boss encounters and rare loot. The Pit is where the most challenging fights are staged.

The finale — The Old Capital

[[zones:oldcapital|The Old Capital]] STUB is the meta-endgame destination, visible on the map from the start but locked until you hold one Hearth-Token of each biome type: a Heirloom Seed (from a completed farm zone), a Pact-Iron token (from a completed mine zone), and a Tidesinger's Pearl (from the harbor). Holding all three unlocks the node and you travel there to renew the Hollow Pact at the place where it was first made — the centennial gathering the founders missed. The precise finale mechanics are intentionally left open until the rest of the game's identity is firmer. The map node and unlock gate exist; there is no travel flow or finale yet. DOC-ONLY
