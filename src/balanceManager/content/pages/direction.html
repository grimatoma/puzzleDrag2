The shape of the game right now

We are building one complete, legible vertical slice first — not the whole kingdom. The slice runs across three towns and showcases most of the game's systems end to end. Everything past Town 3 (more towns, the harbor, the idle hinterland, the finale, keepers, festivals) is parked until this slice plays well.

The core loop

A turn is one chain: drag through three or more adjacent matching tiles (8-direction, no tile reused). At fixed thresholds along the chain an upgraded tile spawns and a resource is credited (floor(chainLength / threshold) per the tile's UPGRADE_THRESHOLDS entry). The board collapses downward and refills. Raw goods refine at buildings — grain → [[resources:flour]] at the [[buildings:mill]], flour → [[resources:bread]] at the [[buildings:bakery]] — and the refined economy is spent to grow the town. The introductory arc (the locked slice)

Town 1 — Hearthwood Vale · farm basics

The tutorial settlement. Teaches the drag-chain, upgrade thresholds, filling orders, the Market, growing the town through the tier ladder, shaping the board with buildings, and the farm refining economy (grain → [[resources:flour]] → [[resources:bread]]). Town 1's specialization is the grain/bread farm.

The expedition to find Town 2

Town 2 is not simply unlocked by a level — you run an expedition to discover it. Raising the wagon at the top of Town 1's tier ladder funds and launches that expedition, bridging the farm cluster to the first mine. This is the forcing function that turns "what do I do next?" into one obvious answer.

Town 2 — the mine & "the combination"

Town 2 teaches mining and, crucially, the combination of the two economies: mine rounds are expeditions whose turns are bought with packed food. The [[buildings:kitchen]] converts farm grain into [[resources:supplies]]; you bring supplies before a run and play until they run out (soft fail — you keep everything gathered). Stone chains yield [[resources:block]], iron chains yield [[resources:iron_bar]]; the [[buildings:workshop]] and [[buildings:forge]] turn metal into better tools. Mine hazards (cave-ins, gas, moles) add urgency the farm never had.

★ The capstone boss (end of Town 2)

Completing Town 2 culminates in a boss battle (reusing an existing boss such as [[bosses:frostmaw]]). It is the dramatic close of the introductory loop and the gate that proves you have mastered farm + mine before the slice opens up. PARTIAL bosses exist; the end-of-Town-2 placement + encounter wiring is the build target.

Town 3 — a richer farm & the rats hazard

The slice closes back on the farm with a second, richer farm hold that introduces farm-side hazard management — mirroring the mine's hazards. Its signature lesson is the rats hazard, handled with Ratcatcher (free moves for rats) and Master Ratcatcher (collect rats together with grass) buildings. It also opens a deeper crop economy and its own distinct specialization. Town 3 = farm + rats is fixed; the exact specialization roster stays draft (see Zones (parked)).

How a zone grows: the per-zone tier ladder WIRED

Each town climbs its own authored ladder of tiers (rung count, plots, costs and unlocks are authored per zone). A tier-up is one town-center ([[buildings:hearth|Hearth]]) upgrade costing escalating local resources, and it does exactly two things: it adds building plots and unlocks the next batch of building types — nothing else. There is deliberately no turn-budget or yield change, so the puzzle math stays untouched. Each (zone, tier) renders a hand-authored Phaser tilemap that grows by appending new plots around the previous town, so a placed building never moves. The home zone reaching its top rung (City) gates the Town 2 expedition. This replaces the old scattered player-level gates legacy. Implementation: tiers[] on each cartography node, the TIER_UP action, and src/ui/town/townMaps.ts. See docs/zone-tier-ladder.html.

How you shape your board: the building loadout

Beyond a town's two staple tiles, the buildings you place decide what the board is. Two mechanics combine into the central choice of the game:

  • Building-gated category spawners. Building a category's producer puts that whole tile category into the spawn pool — a Coop makes the birds category appear (not one specific bird), a Vegetable Patch makes vegetables appear. The per-session picker (Start Farming) then lets you choose which of your unlocked categories to run. The town you build reshapes the board you play.
  • A flexible, demolishable loadout. Some buildings are required for progression, but plots are scarce, so you choose the rest — and you can demolish a building to free its plot and remove its power/category. Because buildings carry powers (spawning a category, boosting yield, combining tiles, granting free moves), the loadout is how you tune your own board. PLANNED open sub-questions: demolish refund, and whether progression-required buildings are non-demolishable.

Settled calls

The design decisions that are no longer up for debate (folded from the old Design decisions page):

  • Remember, not conquer. Progress is rediscovery, not conquest.
  • Soft fail everywhere. No permanent setbacks; the only cost is time and one-shot consumables.
  • No pay-to-win. Premium currency only skips real-time timers; it never buys puzzle advantage.
  • Exactly three biomes (Farm, Mine, Harbor) — though only Farm + Mine appear in this slice; Harbor is parked.
  • Instant crafting. No real-time craft queue or gem-skip.
  • Resources stay per-settlement; coins, tools, workers, level/XP, and knowledge carry globally. A fresh town bootstraps from global coins via its Market.
  • Advancement = the wagon/expedition, built at the top tier — one clear forcing function, not scattered level gates.
