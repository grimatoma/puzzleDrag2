Systems — the rules underneath the catalogs, from a design angle: how a chain becomes goods, how goods become a town, and the knobs that make one board feel different from the next. The live Systems catalog is the authoritative how-it-works; this page is the why and the proposed shape.

PROPOSED  Every system here either feeds or is fed by the single core loop in the Overview. None of them is a second, parallel game.

The board & the chain

A turn is one [[systems:chaining|chain]]: drag through 3+ adjacent matching tiles. At fixed thresholds along the chain an upgraded tile spawns ([[systems:promotion|promotion]]) and a resource is credited; the board collapses and refills. A visit spends a small budget of turns and banks its harvest into that town's stockpile. A chain in one family can also cross-collect a partner family on adjacent tiles, rewarding board-reading.

The spawn pool — buildings decide what the board hands you

The board doesn't hand you everything at once. A tile category only spawns once its building is raised — a coop turns on bird tiles, a [[buildings:sawmill|Sawmill]] deepens trees — and the pool also bends per season and per zone. So your build choices steer the board's output ([[systems:spawn-pool|spawn pool]]). Plots are scarce, so it's a real decision — and you can demolish to re-spec.

The two chain knobs

Two levers — and only two — change a board's felt difficulty without touching the puzzle rules:

KnobWhat moves it
① Chain-length floorHow long a chain must be to collect / promote. Workers lower a family's threshold (toward a floor of 3); a boss raises it. The knob that makes a staffed home board breezy and a boss board tense. wiring open
② Spawn biasWhat the board is likely to hand next — bent by buildings, season, and boss modifiers. How a zone's signature mechanic (tide rows, lit chambers, drifting tiles) expresses itself.

Workers, tools & expeditions

  • Workers — [[buildings:housing|Houses]] produce Villagers; hiring spends a Villager + coins + materials to ease one family's chains. Workers travel with you between towns; a town's materials do not.
  • Tools — single-use board-breakers crafted at the [[buildings:workshop|Workshop]] ([[tools:rake|rake]], [[tools:fertilizer|fertilizer]], [[tools:drill|drill]], [[tools:bomb|bomb]]…) or won from the between-session faucets. The wait hands you tools for free, or you craft them now by spending materials — never a hard time-gate.
  • Expeditions — a mine spends packed food as turns (see Economy → rations).

Hazards & bosses

Hazards are the per-zone environmental pressure — [[hazards:rats|rats]], [[hazards:fire|fire]], [[hazards:cave_in|cave in]], [[hazards:gas_vent|gas vent]], [[hazards:lava|lava]] — expressed through spawn bias and tile overlays. Bosses are the headline challenge beat: each declares board modifiers and a win condition. The coded roster — [[bosses:frostmaw|Frostmaw]], [[bosses:quagmire|Quagmire]], [[bosses:ember_drake|Ember Drake]], [[bosses:old_stoneface|Old Stoneface]], [[bosses:mossback|Mossback]], [[bosses:storm|Storm]] — already maps onto the proposed Atlas.

Integration note: the chain-length and respawn modifiers run live; the tile-overlay modifiers (freeze / rubble / hidden / heat) are written but get wiped by the fresh-board rebuild, so they don't yet reach live play. See Status.

Roguelite boons & keeper paths

On entering a zone you choose a keeper path — Coexist or Drive-Out — drawing board-altering boons from that path's catalog (see the [[keepers:deer_spirit|keepers]] and boons pages). The intent: the choice is a real strategic identity, not just "+X% income." Today the live effects are economic passives; the design adds board-feel boons (diagonal double, cross-collect radius +1, an extra upgrade tier…) so the two paths play differently.
