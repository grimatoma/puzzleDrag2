Economy & balance — the model behind the design: what the currencies are, where value enters and leaves, and the targets we tune toward. Concrete numbers live in code and the catalog pages; this page states the shape, not a copy that could drift.

PROPOSED  Targets and the founding model below are design intent. Exact per-rung costs are code-truth (shown on the live Zones pages and the tier-ladder embeds); the early-game tuning baseline lives in the repo's zones 1–2 baseline doc.

Materials are the real progression gate; coins are a soft secondary one. Building and tier-up costs are resource-only — coins are never spent on construction.

Faucets ↔ sinks

Balance is the conversation between where value enters (faucets) and where it leaves (sinks). Keeping faucets ahead of the sinks that gate them is the no-softlock invariant.

Faucets (value in)Sinks (value out)
[[systems:chaining|Chain]] collection → materials; chain coins (longer chains pay more); the season-end bonus & [[buildings:chapel|Chapel]]; daily rewards, civic tithes, timed depots, boons. Tier-up costs (materials — the main sink); founding caravans; session entry tolls; worker wages & hire ramps; crafting inputs; win-task baskets.

How the board pays

  • Coins scale with chain length (a longer [[systems:chaining|chain]] pays more), weighted by tile value — not by the goods' sell value.
  • Goods accrue per family: every N tiles chained yields one unit, then refine at a station ([[systems:crafting|crafting]]).
  • Founding is a resource caravan — a basket of farm goods early ([[resources:plank|plank]], [[resources:bread|bread]], [[resources:hay_bundle|hay bundle]]), +metal for farther towns — scaling per founding. Not a coin price.
  • Turn budget is a small per-visit allowance (home base ), raised by a [[buildings:granary|Granary]] and doubled by [[tools:fertilizer|fertilizer]].

Rations → mine turns

A mine spends food as expedition turns. Any edible item works — there is no special supply item — and more-processed food buys more turns (a soft efficiency gradient, never a hard requirement): raw produce < crafted staples like [[resources:bread|bread]] < rich crafted like [[resources:cured_meat|cured meat]]. A [[buildings:larder|Larder]] and the mining-camp bonus stack on top.

The value-spread problem

Today the realized value per tile spans far too wide a range (a premium catch like [[resources:pearls|pearls]] dwarfs a humble [[resources:pie|pie]]). Target: compress it toward a modest ceiling so no single tile family dominates income and every board stays worth playing. Two levers move it: a good's sell value and its tiles-per-unit divisor. The playtest harness emits a compression change-list; deep tuning waits on the cross-zone pacing sim.

The price rule — one source of truth

Every sellable good has exactly one sell price. A good must pay the same coins whether the UI classifies it as a "resource" or an "item" — retiring an old fork where the same good could pay wildly different amounts. See [[systems:trade|trade]].

Targets & what's locked

  • Pacing intent: a mid-core player reaches the mid-game (home at its top tier + a second town founded) over a steady week or two, not a single sitting and not a grind.
  • Tuning-locked: the first two-to-three towns (home + the first combination town) — their numbers are the agreed baseline.
  • Provisional: the late arc (towns 4–7 + frontier) is specced but unvalidated until the cross-zone pacing sim crosses boards. See Status.
