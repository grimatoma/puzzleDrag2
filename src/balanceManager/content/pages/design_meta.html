Meta & money — the always-on systems that wrap the core loop: the reasons to come back tomorrow, and the way the game makes money without breaking the cozy promise.

PROPOSED  Retention by warmth, not pressure: every meta system is effort-rewarding and skippable; none time-gates the core loop. The money model is hybrid-casual — optional accelerants and cosmetics, never pay-to-win, no banners.

Story & the Chronicle

The narrative is delivered in beats at milestone moments (founding a town, beating a boss, raising a landmark). Beats are choice-reactive — your picks colour later prose and land in a Chronicle timeline. The arc runs from the cold hearth at [[zones:home]] to the Monument at the capital ([[zones:oldcapital]]); the finale is the capstone beat. The world and cast — [[npcs:mira]], [[npcs:bram]], [[npcs:tomas]], [[npcs:wren]], [[npcs:liss]] — live on the Story page.

Quests, dailies & the streak

  • Daily quests — rotating bite-sized goals (chain N of a family, fill an order, raise a building) that steer attention without forcing a board.
  • Login streak & daily rewards — a streak that ticks once per calendar day and pays escalating rewards (coins, tools, the occasional Villager). One of the between-session faucets that let a returning player go faster for free. half-wired — the reducer ships; a dispatch on app-mount is still needed.

Civic economy — the Town Hall

A return-to-collect civic loop: founded settlements accrue a tithe in real time, claimable at the Town Hall on a cooldown, plus a provisions care-package of tools keyed to your buildings (a [[buildings:mill|Mill]] yields a scythe, a [[buildings:workshop|Workshop]] a seedpack, a [[buildings:forge|Forge]] a lockbox…). Offline accrual works and state persists. implemented

Milestones & the Goals hub

Milestone celebrations mark firsts — first building, first chain-of-10, first town tier, first town founded — once each. The Goals hub is a single panel aggregating the next pull-you-back targets (streak, next achievement / XP tier, next tier-up, active quests), surfacing retention systems that are otherwise buried. to build

Bosses (the challenge wrap)

Bosses are detailed as a system and per-town in the Atlas; as a retention beat they are the headline encounter each zone builds toward, with a story beat and a capstone reward on the win.

The free-to-play money model

Hybrid-casual: fully playable free; spending buys time and shelf-space, never power.

Rewarded ads (opt-in)IAPHybrid expansion
Daily tool-crate; skip a cooldown; boost a win-task; maybe double a run's yield. No banners, ever. Buy Villagers; tool-crate bundles; remove-ads; cosmetics / titles (incl. the finale capstone). Optional expansion villages (extra frontier content) sold as packs; bounded cross-zone exports — no stat-stacking tree.

The line we don't cross. No mechanic may make a paying player stronger on the board than a free one who put in the effort — only faster or prettier. This keeps the "effort-gated, not time-gated" promise honest.
