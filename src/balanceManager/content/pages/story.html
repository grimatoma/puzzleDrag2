The premise

A century ago, the founding settlement of Hearthwood Vale emptied in a single bad winter. Before the last lantern went out, the founders banked the hearth — they did not snuff it — and made a vow: one of their line would return. You are the descendant. You arrive in spring with nothing but the oath and a name.

As your smoke rises, sister settlements across the region — quiet for a hundred years — see it from miles away. Their keepers stir. Their descendants walk home. Your "kingdom" is not built by conquest. It is the rediscovery of a dormant network of vows. The opening is a letter from Wren, the seventy-year-old sole hearthkeeper who has tended the ember alone for fifty-two years: "The ember still burns. I am tired. Come home, if you mean to." Her first act is to hand you the tongs. WIRED (arrival beat)

The world & the Hollow Pact

The Hearthwood is a wide, fertile valley network — mountains to the north and east, marshlands to the south, the Saltspray Sea to the west. It was once a federation of about eleven small holds bound by a founding Charter and a centennial gathering at the Old Capital ([[zones:oldcapital]]). The land was made habitable by the Hollow Folk — slow, lantern-bearing figures who never speak aloud but communicate through tally-marks and stone-arrangements — under a Pact of six terms:

  1. The hearths shall not go out.
  2. The Pact shall be renewed every century at the place of its making.
  3. Sister-holds shall share the labor.
  4. Names shall be kept — what is named remains; what is forgotten, the Hollow Folk reclaim.
  5. The keepers shall be honored.
  6. The Ember shall not be moved.

The abandonment was mundane, not cursed — bad weather, blight, and unlucky timing in one hard winter. Half the population died; the other half scattered. But the founders banked their hearths before leaving. By the time of your arrival, only one still glowed: the one at the Vale, kept by Wren and her line. Your line. The keepers & the central choice

Each settled biome has a land-spirit guardian — a keeper. A settlement's defining decision happens at its outer hearth: Coexist (the keeper stays, the biome keeps its wild magic, you earn Embers) or Drive Out (a boss fight for the hearth, the biome becomes orderly, you earn Ingots). The choice is final per settlement; to walk the other path you found a new settlement of that type. Bosses such as [[bosses:frostmaw]] and [[bosses:old_stoneface]] are keeper-style encounters, not gates. The finale

The Old Capital is the meta-endgame, reached only when you hold one Hearth-Token of each type (a Heirloom Seed from a farm, Pact-Iron from a mine, a Tidesinger's Pearl from a harbor). There, the journey reads back your record — which settlement you founded first, which keepers you Coexisted versus Drove Out, which arcs you completed — and gates the Charter renewal accordingly. The precise mechanics are intentionally left open until the rest of the game's identity is firmer. DOC-ONLY (the unlock gate and map node exist; there is no travel flow or finale yet)
