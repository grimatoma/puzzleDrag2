Tiles are the pieces you drag across the board. They live on the 6×6 grid and never enter your inventory — when you complete a chain, the tiles vanish and their chain credits a resource instead. Each tile belongs to a biome and a family (grass, grain, trees, birds, vegetables, fruits, flowers, herd animals, cattle, mounts, mine ores, fish) and the family determines which resource the chain produces.

The board fills from the farm, mine, or harbor pool depending on which zone you are playing. Chaining enough tiles of the same type triggers an upgrade: the game spawns a higher-tier tile in its place, giving you the chance to climb the chain ladder in a single session. Representative farm tiles include [[tiles:tile_grain_wheat]] (the bread pipeline's starting point) and [[tiles:tile_tree_oak]] (the source of [[resources:plank]]).
