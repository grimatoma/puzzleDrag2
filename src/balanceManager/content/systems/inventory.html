Inventory is where the goods you produce wait to be used. Each settlement keeps its own per-zone store: resources credited by [[systems:chaining|chains]] land here, [[systems:crafting|crafting]] draws its inputs from here, and [[systems:trade|trade]] sells out of here.

Storage isn't unlimited. Each resource has a soft cap, and production beyond it overflows rather than vanishing — overflow is collected separately so a long, lucky chain is never simply wasted. Raising the [[buildings:granary]] lifts your cap (and adds a turn), so storage upgrades are what let a stronger board actually translate into a bigger stockpile.

Because caps shape what's worth making, inventory quietly governs pacing: when a resource is capped, the smart play shifts to spending it through [[systems:crafting|crafting]] or [[systems:trade|trade]] rather than chaining still more of the same.
