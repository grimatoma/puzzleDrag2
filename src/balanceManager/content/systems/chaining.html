Chaining is the single move the whole game is built on. Drag a path through three or more adjacent tiles of the same family and they merge: the tiles are consumed and the chain pays out — a resource of that family is credited to your [[systems:inventory|inventory]]. Wheat chains credit [[resources:flour]], stone chains credit [[resources:block]], and every tile family has its own produced good.

Length is the lever. A longer chain pays more — yield scales with how many tiles you swept — and once a chain is long enough it also triggers [[systems:promotion]], leaving an upgraded tile behind. Because every later system is downstream of the goods a chain produces, reading the board for long chains is the core skill the game rewards.

What you have available to chain at any moment is set by the [[systems:spawn-pool]]; what your goods are worth afterward is set by [[systems:crafting]] and [[systems:trade]]. Tools such as the [[tools:axe|Axe]] can also clear tiles in bulk when no good chain is on offer.
