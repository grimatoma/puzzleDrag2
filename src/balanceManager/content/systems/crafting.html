Crafting is how raw board goods become valuable. A recipe takes a set of input resources and produces an output, but only at the right station — a building you've constructed. Grain becomes [[resources:flour]] at the [[buildings:mill]], flour becomes [[resources:bread]] at the [[buildings:bakery]], and the [[buildings:forge]] opens the metal-smithing tier. Once the station is up and you hold the inputs, crafting is instant.

Recipes form production lines: the output of one is the input of the next, so a settlement's buildings chain together into a pipeline from board tile to high-value good. The [[buildings:kitchen]] turns farm grain into [[resources:supplies]] that buy expedition turns; the [[buildings:smokehouse]] and [[buildings:larder]] extend the food line further.

Crafting draws down your [[systems:inventory|stored resources]] and feeds [[systems:trade|trade]] — refined goods sell for far more than the raw inputs, and many townsfolk orders ask for crafted items specifically. Workers like the [[workers:baker|Baker]] cut what a recipe costs — each Baker shaves a flour off the bread recipe.
