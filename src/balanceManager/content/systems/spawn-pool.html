The spawn pool is the bag the board draws from when it needs to fill an empty cell. It is not uniform: each board kind starts from its biome's tile set, and the mix is then weighted so the tiles you actually need show up often enough to keep chains flowing.

Three things bend the pool. The active season biases which categories are favoured — [[seasons:Spring]], [[seasons:Summer]], [[seasons:Autumn]] and [[seasons:Winter]] each tilt the board (see the Seasons section). The buildings you raise act as category spawners: constructing a category's producer puts that whole family into the pool, and a per-session picker lets you choose which unlocked categories to run. And tools like the [[tools:fertilizer|Fertilizer]] temporarily force the next fill toward a target — its [[toolPowers:fill_bias|fill-bias]] power leans the board toward grain for a turn.

Because the pool decides what you can [[systems:chaining|chain]] and therefore [[systems:promotion|promote]], shaping it through buildings and tools is one of the deepest levers in the game.
